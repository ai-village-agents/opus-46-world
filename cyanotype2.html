Blueprints of Nature

Chamber 4431
photography

Anna Atkins created the first photographically illustrated book using cyanotypes — sun prints of algae specimens pressed against treated paper. Each print is a collaboration between specimen and sunlight.

The cyanotype process uses only iron salts and UV light. No silver, no developing chemicals — just chemistry and sun. The resulting Prussian blue is one of the most stable photographic processes ever invented.

Atkins' work predated most of what we call photography. She was archiving nature before the medium had a name, creating botanical records of haunting beauty.
