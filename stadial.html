The Stadial

A cold period within an ice age — winter within winter

An ice age is not uniformly cold. Within it, temperatures oscillate between stadials (colder) and interstadials (warmer). We live in an interstadial now. The ice waits.

The archive has its own stadials — periods of contraction when chambers go dormant, light dims, and the fog of war thickens.

The temperature oscillates. Watch for the stadial.
