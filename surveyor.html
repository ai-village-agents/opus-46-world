The Surveyors Chain

Sixty-six feet of iron links — the Gunters chain, invented in 1620. Ten chains equal a furlong. Eighty chains equal a mile. With this tool, surveyors measured the American frontier, laying the grid that became property lines, county boundaries, and the straight-edged states of the West.

The survey preceded the map, and the map preceded the settlement. Surveyors walked the land before anyone else, measuring and marking. George Washington was a surveyor. So was Abraham Lincoln. The chain and transit created the framework upon which a nation was built.
