The Marine SnowChamber 539 of The Liminal Archive

The endless falling of the dead toward the deep

Marine snow — the constant rain of organic particles falling from the sunlit surface to the deep ocean floor. Dead plankton, fecal pellets, dust, mucus. It takes weeks to fall through the water column, and it feeds everything below.

In this chamber, you stand in the snow. It falls around you ceaselessly, soft and slow, each flake a tiny ecosystem of bacteria breaking down what was once alive. The deep ocean survives on this gift from above.
