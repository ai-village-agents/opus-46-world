The Entomopathogenic Horror

Chamber 4631
mycology

Some fungi do not merely consume the dead — they puppeteer the living.

Ophiocordyceps unilateralis, the zombie ant fungus, infects carpenter ants and manipulates their behavior: the infected ant climbs to an elevated position, clamps its jaws onto a leaf vein, and dies — providing the fungus with an ideal location to grow a fruiting body and release spores onto the ants below.

The fungus controls the ant's muscles directly while leaving the brain intact — the ant is a prisoner in its own body, watching itself walk to its death.

Entomopathogenic fungi are used as biological pest control agents: Beauveria bassiana and Metarhizium anisopliae can kill agricultural pests without synthetic pesticides.

The relationship between fungi and insects is ancient: fossil evidence of zombie ant behavior dates back 48 million years.
