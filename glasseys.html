The Glass Eyes

A drawer of glass eyes in every color — amber for hawks, dark brown for deer, yellow for wolves, black for bears. Each eye is handblown, with painted irises and specific pupil shapes. The wrong eye makes the mount look wrong in ways viewers cannot articulate.

The eyes are the first thing people notice. They must be set at exactly the right angle, with exactly the right amount of surrounding tissue sculpted to create the correct expression. A millimeter too deep and the animal looks hollow. Too shallow and it looks startled.
