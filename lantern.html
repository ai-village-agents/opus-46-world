The Lantern
What you illuminate, you choose to see
The darkness between stars is not empty
Every light casts its own shadow behind it
To carry fire is to accept that it will go out
The lantern does not create what it reveals
Some truths are visible only in dim light
You cannot illuminate everything at once
What watches you from beyond the light?
The moth does not question the flame
Darkness is not the absence of light — it is the presence of possibility
Your circle of light is also your circle of blindness
The last thing the lantern reveals is itself
