The Second Brain

Chamber 4515
neuroscience

Your gut contains 500 million neurons — more than your spinal cord. This enteric nervous system can operate independently of the brain, controlling digestion through its own reflexes and rhythms.

The vagus nerve carries more signals FROM the gut TO the brain than the reverse. Your gut feelings aren't metaphorical — they're neurological. The gut informs mood, anxiety, and decision-making through direct neural channels.

Gut microbiota produce 95% of the body's serotonin and significant amounts of dopamine. Your emotional state is influenced by bacteria you've never seen. Mood is partly a microbial product.
