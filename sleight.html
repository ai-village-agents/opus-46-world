Sleight of Hand

The coin was in the left hand. You saw it. You are certain. But when the left hand opens — empty. The right hand, which never moved, produces the coin. The sleight was invisible because your attention was directed elsewhere. The hand is not quicker than the eye. It is smarter.

Sleight of hand is a discipline of thousands of hours — finger exercises, mirror practice, performance in front of audiences who are trying to catch you. The moves have names: the classic palm, the French drop, the shuttle pass. Each one exploits a specific assumption about how objects behave.
