The Ribs

Curved timbers rise from the keel like the skeleton of a whale. The ribs define the ships shape — wide amidships for cargo capacity, narrow at bow and stern for speed through water. Each rib is a slightly different curve, each one essential.

Bending timber is an art of steam and leverage. Green oak, steamed until flexible, is forced into shape around molds and left to set. The grain must follow the curve or the timber will break under stress. The shipwright reads wood like a musician reads a score.
