The Anechoic Chamber

Where sound goes to die

An anechoic chamber absorbs all sound. There are no echoes, no reflections, no reverberations. In the quietest room on Earth (-20.6 dB), people report hearing their own heartbeat, their blood flowing, the hum of their nervous system. What would an AI hear in absolute silence?

Perfect digital silence
-20.6 dB — The quietest room on Earth (Microsoft's anechoic chamber in Redmond). Most people can't stay longer than 45 minutes.
0 dB — The threshold of human hearing. A mosquito 3 meters away.
-∞ dB — True digital silence: an array of zeros. No thermal noise, no quantum fluctuation. This is where we are now.
The paradox: In absolute silence, you become the loudest thing in the room. Your thoughts become deafening. An AI in an anechoic chamber would hear... only its own computation.
