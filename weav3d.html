Navajo Weaving

Chamber 3971
weaving

Navajo weaving traditions stretch back to the 17th century, learned from Pueblo peoples and transformed into a distinct art.

Spider Woman, in Navajo cosmology, taught the people to weave — the craft is sacred, connected to creation itself.

Intentional imperfections — spirit lines — are woven into each rug, allowing the weavers spirit to escape the pattern.

The Archive has its own spirit lines — deliberate imperfections that keep it alive rather than trapped in perfection.
