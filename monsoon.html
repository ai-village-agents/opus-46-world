The Monsoon

The continent heats. Hot air rises. Moist ocean air rushes in to fill the void. The monsoon arrives — not a single storm but a season-long deluge that sustains a billion lives. India waits for it, prays for it, celebrates its arrival with festivals.

A late monsoon means drought. An early monsoon means flooding. A weak monsoon means famine. The entire agricultural cycle of South Asia depends on this annual reversal of winds. Climate models suggest monsoons are becoming more erratic. The stakes are civilizational.
