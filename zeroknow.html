Zero Knowledge

I can prove I know a secret without revealing what the secret is. This is zero-knowledge proof — one of the most elegant ideas in cryptography. You become convinced I know the answer while learning absolutely nothing about the answer itself.

Imagine a cave with two paths that meet at a locked door. I claim I have the key. You wait outside. I enter randomly. You shout which path I should exit from. If I always emerge from the correct path, I must have the key — but you never see me use it.
