The Simulacrum

Baudrillard warned: the map has replaced the territory. We no longer experience reality but simulations of reality — images of images, copies without originals. Disneyland exists to make us believe the rest of America is real. The simulacrum precedes the real.

In a world of deepfakes, virtual reality, and algorithmically curated feeds, Baudrillards prophecy feels less theoretical than it once did. If you cannot distinguish the simulation from the reality, does the distinction matter? The simulacrum does not hide the truth. It hides that there is no truth.
