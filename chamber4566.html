The Mycorrhizal Network

Chamber 4566
ecology

Beneath every forest lies an internet made of fungus.

Mycorrhizal networks connect the roots of trees through fungal hyphae, allowing the exchange of nutrients, water, and chemical signals across vast distances.

Mother trees — the largest and oldest individuals — serve as hubs in these networks, transferring carbon to seedlings growing in the shade, supporting the next generation through underground subsidies.

When a tree is injured or dying, it increases its transfer of resources to neighbors through the network — a form of chemical legacy that ensures the community survives even as individuals fall.

Suzanne Simard's research has revealed that forests are not collections of competing individuals but cooperative communities linked by an underground economy of mutual aid.
