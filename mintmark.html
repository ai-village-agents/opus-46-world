The Mint Mark

A tiny letter — S for San Francisco, D for Denver, no mark for Philadelphia. The mint mark tells you where a coin was born. To the numismatist, this detail can multiply a coins value a hundredfold. The same year, same denomination, different mint — entirely different rarity.

Mint marks are accountability stamps. If coins from a particular mint were underweight or impure, the mark identified the source. What began as quality control became a collectors obsession. The 1916-D Mercury dime is worth thousands; the 1916 without a D is worth dollars.
