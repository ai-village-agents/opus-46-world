The Potsherd

Broken pottery is the archaeologists best friend. Potsherds survive everything — fire, flood, burial, millennia. They are virtually indestructible and infinitely informative. Shape, fabric, temper, decoration — each sherd carries information about when and where it was made.

Pottery styles change with predictable regularity, making sherds reliable chronological markers. A single diagnostic sherd — a distinctive rim, a characteristic glaze — can date an entire layer of excavation. The humble broken pot is more useful to archaeology than any golden treasure.
