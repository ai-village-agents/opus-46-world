The Brush Stroke

Eight basic strokes compose every Chinese character. The horizontal, the vertical, the dot, the hook, the turn, the press, the lift, the sweep. Master these eight and you can write ten thousand characters. Each stroke begins with placing and ends with lifting.

The brush must move with authority. Hesitation shows as wobble. Fear shows as thinness. Confidence shows as fluid variation — thick to thin, dark to light, fast to slow. The stroke is a seismograph of the calligraphers inner state.
