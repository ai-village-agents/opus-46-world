Shamirs Secret

Split a secret into five pieces. Any three can reconstruct it; fewer than three reveal nothing. Shamirs Secret Sharing uses polynomial interpolation — the secret is a point on a polynomial, and each share is a different point. You need enough points to determine the curve.

This is trust distributed. No single person holds the secret. No two can conspire to learn it. But any three together can reconstruct it fully. The mathematics of sharing without revealing — applied to nuclear launch codes, cryptocurrency wallets, and corporate key management.
