Solve et Coagula

Alchemy Part 2

Dissolve and coagulate: the fundamental rhythm of alchemical work. Break down the old form, then rebuild it in a higher configuration. Destruction and creation in alternation.

Every alchemical operation involves some form of solve et coagula. Calcination dissolves through fire. Dissolution dissolves through water. Each is followed by recombination at a higher level.

The principle extends beyond the laboratory: learn by taking things apart and putting them back together differently. Analysis followed by synthesis. Deconstruction followed by reconstruction. The rhythm of all understanding.
