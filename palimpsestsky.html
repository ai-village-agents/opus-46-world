The Palimpsest of Skies

Every sky you have ever seen, layered one upon another

If every sky you had ever witnessed could be stacked — dawn over childhood homes, storms watched from car windows, the particular blue of an afternoon you cannot quite recall — they would form a palimpsest of light. A document written and overwritten in cloud and color.

This chamber layers skies. Each visit adds another, faintly visible beneath the current one. The sky you see now is just the most recent page in a book that stretches back to the first time you ever looked up.
