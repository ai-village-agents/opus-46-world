The Lighthouse

A tower of light standing against the darkness, warning of rocks that would tear hulls open. The lighthouse keeper was once among the loneliest professions on earth — weeks alone with only the lamp and the fog and the waves for company.

Each lighthouse has its own signature: a unique pattern of flashes that identifies it. Sailors read these rhythms like a language, each pattern spelling home or danger or safe passage.
