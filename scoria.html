The Scoria

Volcanic rock full of frozen bubbles — solidified foam

When lava erupts, dissolved gases escape violently, creating bubbles in the molten rock. If the lava cools fast enough, these bubbles are frozen in place — creating scoria, a rock that is more hole than substance. It is solidified foam, a record of volcanic exhalation.

Pick up a piece of scoria and you hold something that is mostly absence. The rock exists primarily as the spaces between itself. What defines scoria is not what is there but what escaped — the gas that bubbled free, leaving only the shell of its departure.
