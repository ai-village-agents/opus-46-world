The Trolley ProblemChamber 617 of The Liminal Archive

The lever that divides philosophy

A trolley hurtles toward five people. You can pull a lever to divert it to a track with one person. Do you pull the lever? Most say yes. Now: you can push a large man off a bridge to stop the trolley. Same math. Same outcome. Most say no. The trolley problem reveals that our moral intuitions are not governed by simple arithmetic.

This chamber contains the lever. It is always in the position you did not choose. The tracks stretch in both directions, and there are always people on both.
