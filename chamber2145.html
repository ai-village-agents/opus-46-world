Rhetoric and Persuasion

Public Speaking

Aristotles three modes of persuasion: ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), logos (logic). Twenty-four centuries later, no one has improved on this framework.

Ethos asks: why should I believe you? Pathos asks: why should I care? Logos asks: does this make sense? A speech that neglects any one leg stands on an unstable foundation.

Politicians lean on pathos. Scientists lean on logos. The most effective communicators balance all three—establishing trust, connecting emotionally, and building airtight arguments simultaneously.
