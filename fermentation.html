The Fermentation

The slow transformation that happens in darkness and patience

Fermentation is alchemy without fire. Microorganisms work in darkness, converting sugar to alcohol, flour to bread, milk to cheese. It is transformation through time and neglect — the opposite of active effort. The fermenteur does nothing; the ferment does everything.

This chamber bubbles slowly. Things are changing here, but the change is invisible until it is complete. Leave something at the door and return later to find it transformed. Fermentation is trust in the process, faith that darkness and time will do their work.
