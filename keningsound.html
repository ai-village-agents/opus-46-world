The Kenning

The Old Norse art of naming things by what they are not

In Old Norse poetry, the sea was not called the sea. It was the whale-road. Gold was not gold — it was Sif's hair. Battle was not battle — it was the storm of swords. A kenning names a thing by circling around it, approaching it from the side, refusing direct address.

This chamber practices the art of indirection. Everything here is described by what it resembles, what it does, what it reminds you of — never by what it is. The chamber itself might be called the thought-house, the wonder-hall, the memory-cave.
