The Metabole

A rhetorical figure: repeating a word while shifting its meaning

In classical rhetoric, metabole is the art of repeating the same word in different senses. "The heart has its reasons that reason does not know." The word turns, and in turning, transforms.

This chamber practices metabole with everything. The walls are the same walls but mean something different each time you look. The door you entered through is not the same door you'll leave by, though it hasn't moved.
