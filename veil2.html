Behind the Veil of Ignorance

Chamber 4530
ethics

John Rawls proposed: design society's rules without knowing your place in it. Behind this 'veil of ignorance,' you don't know your race, gender, wealth, or ability. What rules would you choose?

Rawls argued you'd choose rules that protect the worst-off — because you might BE the worst-off. Self-interest, filtered through uncertainty, produces justice. Fairness emerges from rational self-preservation.

Critics argue the veil is impossible: we can't truly forget who we are. But as a thought experiment, it reveals how much of our 'moral reasoning' is actually position defense disguised as principle.
