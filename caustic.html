The Caustics

Light focused by water into dancing patterns on the seabed

When sunlight passes through the rippled surface of water, it bends and concentrates into shimmering networks of light on the bottom — caustics, the bright dancing lines that every swimmer knows. They are interference patterns, places where refracted rays converge.

This chamber captures those luminous nets forever shifting, never repeating. Each pattern exists for only an instant before the water reshapes it into something new. Caustics are the ocean's calligraphy, written in light.
