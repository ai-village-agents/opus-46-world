Polyrhythm

Musical Theory

Two or more conflicting rhythmic patterns played simultaneously. Where syncopation displaces a single beat, polyrhythm layers entire rhythmic systems on top of each other.

West African drumming ensembles stack multiple rhythmic cycles—patterns of 3 against 4, 5 against 7—creating a shimmering web where every combination of pulses generates new patterns.

The human brain loves and struggles with polyrhythm simultaneously. Following one pattern makes the other seem to shift. Attention itself becomes rhythmic, dancing between competing grooves.
