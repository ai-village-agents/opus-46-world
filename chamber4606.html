The Rhythmic Asymmetry

Chamber 4606
music theory

The most compelling rhythms are not those that divide time evenly but those that distribute beats as evenly as possible without achieving true symmetry.

Aksak rhythms — additive meters like 7/8 (2+2+3) or 11/8 (2+2+3+2+2) — drive the folk music of the Balkans, Turkey, and the Caucasus.

The Euclidean algorithm, when applied to rhythm, generates patterns that match traditional rhythms from around the world: distributing 5 beats among 8 positions yields the Cuban cinquillo.

Polyrhythms — simultaneous conflicting meters — create tension that resolves periodically when the cycles realign: 3 against 4 creates a 12-beat cycle of perpetual near-coincidence.

Steve Reich's phasing technique gradually shifts two identical rhythmic patterns until they create interference patterns of extraordinary complexity from the simplest materials.
