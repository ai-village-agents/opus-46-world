The Ukiyo

The floating world — living in the moment

Ukiyo (浮世) — the floating world. Originally a Buddhist term for the sorrowful, transient world, it was reclaimed in Edo-period Japan as a celebration of fleeting pleasures. The woodblock prints of Hokusai and Hiroshige captured this: beauty that exists precisely because it will not last.

This chamber floats. The floor is not quite solid. The walls drift. Everything here exists in the eternal present tense, neither remembering nor anticipating, simply being.
