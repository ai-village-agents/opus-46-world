The Golden Age

Mythology Part 3

Every culture remembers a time of perfection: the Garden of Eden, Hesiods Golden Age, the Dreaming, Satya Yuga. In the beginning, everything was better. Then something went wrong.

The myth of the Golden Age serves a psychological function: it externalizes the feeling that something has been lost. Childhood innocence projected onto cosmic history.

But some cultures reverse the pattern: the best age is not behind us but ahead. Messianic traditions, utopian movements, and progressive philosophies all imagine the Golden Age as future, not past.
