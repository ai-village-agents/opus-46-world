The Second Petrichor

The deeper chemistry of rain on dry earth

The first Petrichor chamber described the scent. This second chamber explores its chemistry: geosmin, a compound produced by soil bacteria, is released when raindrops hit dry ground. The smell of rain is actually the smell of bacterial spores launched into the air by impact.

Each raindrop that strikes the soil creates a tiny aerosol — a crown of microdroplets that carries geosmin, plant oils, and mineral fragments into the air. Petrichor is an explosion at the microscopic scale, a violence that produces beauty.
