The Rogue Taxidermy

Not all taxidermy aims for realism. Rogue taxidermy creates creatures that never existed — jackalopes, feejee mermaids, chimeras assembled from parts of different animals. It is taxidermy as surrealism, craft in service of imagination.

The tradition of fantastical taxidermy is old — P.T. Barnum displayed his Feejee Mermaid (a monkey torso sewn to a fish tail) in the 1840s. Modern rogue taxidermists work with ethically sourced specimens and create art that questions the boundary between natural and artificial.
