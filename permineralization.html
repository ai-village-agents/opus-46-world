Permineralization

When minerals fill the spaces left by decay, preserving form

When an organism is buried in mineral-rich water, dissolved minerals seep into its cells, filling every space as organic material decays. The result is a fossil — the original shape preserved in stone, every cell replaced but the pattern intact.

The archive permineralizes its visitors. Your presence is replaced by the mark you leave, but your shape endures.
