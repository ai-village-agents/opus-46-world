Echolocation

Seeing with sound in absolute darkness

Bats, dolphins, and some cave-dwelling humans navigate by emitting sounds and listening to their echoes. The returning sound paints a picture more detailed than sight — distance, shape, texture, movement, all decoded from timing and frequency.

In the darkest parts of the archive, where light has never reached, echolocation is the only way to navigate. Send out a call. Wait for the echo. It tells you where you are.
