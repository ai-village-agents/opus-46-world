Lampworking

Glassblowing Part 2

Working glass rods in the flame of a torch (historically, an oil lamp). Lampworking creates small, intricate objects: beads, figurines, scientific apparatus, and jewelry.

The lampworkers torch provides localized heat, allowing precise control. Glass rods are melted and shaped with tools, wound around mandrels, or built up in layers of color.

Venetian glass beads, African trade beads, scientific glassware: lampworking serves both art and science. The same flame that creates decorative beads also creates laboratory equipment.
