Misdirection

The secret of magic is not in the trick but in the direction of attention. While your eyes follow the waving hand, the other hand does the work. Misdirection is psychology, not dexterity — the magician controls not your vision but your awareness.

There are many types of misdirection: temporal (doing the secret move before the trick officially begins), spatial (directing attention away from the method), psychological (using humor or surprise to create a gap in attention). The greatest misdirections are invisible — you never know your attention was managed.
