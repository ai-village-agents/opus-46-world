The Virga

Rain that evaporates before reaching the ground

High above the desert, clouds release their rain — but the air below is so dry that every drop evaporates before it can touch the earth. This phantom rain, called virga, hangs in curtains beneath clouds: promises of water that dissolve into absence.

Virga is the ultimate unfulfilled intention. The sky means to rain, the clouds do their part, but the distance between intention and arrival is too great. The dry air intervenes, converting water back to vapor, and the ground remains parched beneath veils of almost-rain.
