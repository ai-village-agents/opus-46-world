The Box Pleat Grid

A grid of perpendicular creases divides the paper into a matrix of squares. From this grid, any form can be collapsed — the box pleat method is origami s universal scaffold, the foundation upon which modern complex folding is built.

The grid allows the designer to allocate paper precisely. Each feature of the final model — each leg, wing, antenna, and tail — gets its own region of the grid. The mathematics are rigorous. The results are magical. From a grid of squares: a life-sized insect with jointed legs.
