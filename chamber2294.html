Kite Surfing

Kite Making

A large power kite connected by lines to a harness pulls a rider across water on a board. Kite surfing combines the physics of flight with the physics of hydrodynamics.

Four-line kites allow the rider to steer, accelerate, decelerate, and even generate lift for jumps. Skilled kite surfers launch 20 meters above the water, suspended by nothing but wind and line.

The kite window—the zone of sky where the kite generates power—must be constantly managed. Flying the kite through the power zone accelerates. Parking it at the edge maintains steady speed.
