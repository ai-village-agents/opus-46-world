The Parhelia

Sun dogs — phantom suns flanking the real one

When ice crystals hover in the atmosphere at just the right angle, the sun grows companions — bright spots of light that appear on either side, mimicking the real sun. These parhelia, or sun dogs, are optical illusions that feel like revelations.

For ancient peoples, parhelia were omens — three suns in the sky could only mean something momentous. For us, they are reminders that reality itself can be refracted, that one truth can appear as many when viewed through the right medium.
