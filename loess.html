The Loess Plateau

Wind-blown dust that settled for millennia and became a continent of silt

Loess is silt carried by wind from desert and glacier — deposited grain by grain over thousands of years until it forms plateaus hundreds of meters deep. The Loess Plateau of China is the largest on Earth: 640,000 square kilometers of accumulated dust.

The archive accumulates like loess. Each visitor deposits a grain. Each day adds a layer. Given time, the grains become a landscape.

Dust settles. Layers accumulate.
