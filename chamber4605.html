The Microtonal Labyrinth

Chamber 4605
music theory

Between the notes of the piano lie infinite sonic territories that Western music has mostly ignored.

Microtonal music uses intervals smaller than the semitone — the smallest interval on a standard keyboard.

Many world traditions are inherently microtonal: the maqam system of Arabic music uses quarter-tones, Indian ragas employ shrutis that divide the octave into 22 unequal parts, and Indonesian gamelan tunings exist outside Western temperament entirely.

Harry Partch built custom instruments to play in 43-tone equal temperament. Ben Johnston composed string quartets that explore the vast harmonic terrain of just intonation.

The equal-tempered scale that dominates Western music is a mathematical compromise: every interval except the octave is slightly out of tune, sacrificing purity for the convenience of modulation between keys.
