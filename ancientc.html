The Ancient Coin

Alexander the Great stares at you from a coin minted 2,300 years ago. His portrait, idealized and divine, was stamped on coins across his empire — from Greece to India. Holding this coin, you hold something he might have held. The past is tangible.

Ancient coins are history you can touch. A Roman sestertius paid a soldier. A Greek drachma bought a days labor. An Islamic dinar funded scholarship in Baghdad. Each coin circulated through hands now dust, in markets now ruins, in civilizations now memory.
