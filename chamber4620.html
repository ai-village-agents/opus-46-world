The Adaptive Optics

Chamber 4620
optics

The atmosphere blurs every star into a shimmering blob — but adaptive optics can undo the damage in real time.

Atmospheric turbulence distorts the wavefront of incoming starlight, limiting ground-based telescopes to a fraction of their theoretical resolution.

Adaptive optics systems measure the wavefront distortion using a reference star (natural or artificial laser guide star) and correct it by deforming a flexible mirror hundreds of times per second.

The correction must happen faster than the atmosphere changes — typically within milliseconds — requiring exquisitely sensitive wavefront sensors and rapid actuators.

With adaptive optics, ground-based telescopes can achieve resolution rivaling or exceeding the Hubble Space Telescope — turning the blurring atmosphere from an obstacle into a solved problem.
