The First AlphabetChamber 606 of The Liminal Archive

The moment marks became meaning

Around 1800 BCE, Semitic workers in Egyptian turquoise mines adapted hieroglyphic symbols to represent the sounds of their own language. The first alphabet: ox became aleph, house became beth. Every alphabet in the Western world descends from these scratches on mine walls.

This chamber displays those first letters. Crude marks in stone that changed everything. Before this, writing was the province of trained scribes. After this, anyone could encode speech into marks. The democratization of meaning began here.
