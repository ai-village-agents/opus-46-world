The Negative Space

Chamber 4051
sculpture

Henry Moore carved holes into his figures, making absence as sculptural as presence. The void through a torso frames the landscape behind it, making the viewer aware of the space the sculpture inhabits.

Barbara Hepworth took this further, piercing her abstract forms with windows that invited the viewer to look through the sculpture rather than at it. The hole became as meaningful as the stone.
