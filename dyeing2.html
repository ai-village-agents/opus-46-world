The Dye Vat

Color comes from unexpected sources. Tyrian purple from sea snails. Indigo from fermented leaves. Cochineal red from crushed insects. Saffron yellow from crocus stamens. Before synthetic dyes, color was precious — sometimes literally worth more than gold.

The dye vat bubbles with chemistry ancient and precise. Temperature, pH, timing, mordants — each variable affects the final color. The same dye bath can produce different shades depending on when the fiber is added and removed. The dyer works by experience, not formula.
