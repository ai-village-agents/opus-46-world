The Dark Stage

Chamber 4270
theater

On dark nights — when no performance is scheduled — the theater sits empty. A single light, called the ghost light, burns center stage. Tradition says it wards off ghosts. Practically, it prevents accidents in the dark.

The ghost light is theatrically perfect: a lone light on an empty stage, keeping vigil against darkness and spirits. Every theater has its ghosts — past performances that linger in the space, audience laughter that echoes in empty seats.
