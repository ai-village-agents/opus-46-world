The Drumlin

A hill shaped by the belly of a glacier

Smooth and whale-shaped, this hill was molded by ice pressing down from above — a form created by weight and patience. Drumlins always point in the direction the glacier was traveling, like arrows frozen in the landscape.

Stand atop this gentle mound and you can read the ice's intentions across millennia. Every drumlin is a sentence in the glacier's autobiography, recording its passage through a land that yielded to its will.
