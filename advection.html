The Advection Fog

Warm air flowing over cold ground — fog that travels horizontally

When warm, moist air slides over cold ground or cold water, it chills rapidly and condenses into fog. Unlike radiation fog that forms in place, advection fog moves — it flows horizontally like a slow river of cloud, pouring over hills and through valleys.

San Francisco's famous fog is advection fog: warm Pacific air meeting the cold California Current. It rolls through the Golden Gate like a fluid, filling the bay with a river of white that swallows bridges and hills and sounds.
