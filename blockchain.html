The Blockchain

Each block contains a hash of the previous block. Alter any block and its hash changes, which changes the next block, and the next, cascading forward — the entire chain screams that it has been tampered with. Integrity through interdependence.

The blockchain is a ledger that no one controls and everyone can verify. Its most famous application is cryptocurrency, but the principle is broader: any record that needs to be tamper-evident and decentralized. The math is simple. The implications are revolutionary.
