The Cosmic WebChamber 536 of The Liminal Archive

The largest structure that exists

On the largest scales, the universe is not uniform. Matter collects along vast filaments — threads of dark matter and gas stretching hundreds of millions of light-years, connecting galaxy clusters like dew on a spider's web.

This chamber shows you that web. Each node is a galaxy cluster containing thousands of galaxies, each galaxy containing hundreds of billions of stars. The voids between filaments are the emptiest places in existence — regions where almost nothing has ever been.

You are seeing the skeleton of reality itself.
