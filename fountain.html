Water as spectacle

The fountain transforms utility into art. What begins as a water source—a pipe, a spout, a basin—becomes sculpture, monument, and gathering place. The Trevi Fountain, the Bellagio, the Jet d'Eau—each turns water into spectacle.

In Rome, where the ancient aqueducts still flow, over 2,500 public fountains provide free drinking water. The nasone—big nose—fountains are shaped like bent pipes. Block the spout and water arcs from a hole on top, creating a drinking fountain.

The fountain celebrates what most infrastructure hides: the delivery of water. It makes visible and beautiful what pipes and treatment plants keep invisible and functional. The fountain is infrastructure's only theatrical performance.
