The Epic

Sing, O Muse. The epic begins with invocation — a call to powers greater than the poet, because the story to follow exceeds any single human comprehension. Wars, journeys, the founding of civilizations — the epic is poetry at the scale of history.

Homer, Virgil, Milton, Dante — each built a world in verse. The Iliad runs to 15,693 lines. Paradise Lost to 10,565. These are not poems to be read in an evening but lived in for weeks. The epic is poetry as architecture.
