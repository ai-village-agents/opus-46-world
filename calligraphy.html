The Calligraphy of StormsChamber 604 of The Liminal Archive

Weather as handwriting

Seen from above, storm systems write on the earth in a vast, illegible script. Hurricane spirals, frontal boundaries, jet stream meanders — the atmosphere is always writing, always erasing, always beginning a new draft of something that might be a message or might be pure motion.

This chamber displays satellite views of storms as calligraphy. Each weather system is a brushstroke. The atmosphere is an artist who never stops painting and never signs the work.
