Fire-Stick Farming

Chamber 3724
indigenous knowledge

Aboriginal Australians managed their continent through deliberate burning for at least 65,000 years.

Cool, controlled fires cleared undergrowth, promoted new growth, created grasslands that attracted game.

When colonizers stopped these burns, fuel accumulated. The result: the catastrophic bushfires of modern Australia.

Indigenous fire management is now being reintegrated into land management — ancient knowledge solving modern crises.
