Home Sign

When a deaf child is born to hearing parents who do not sign, something remarkable happens: the child invents their own language. Home signs — gestural systems created spontaneously by deaf children — demonstrate that the language instinct is so powerful it will create language from nothing.

Home signs develop grammar. They develop consistency. They develop complexity. A single child, without any linguistic input in an accessible modality, will build a communication system with systematic rules. Language is not learned. It is grown.
