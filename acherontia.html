The Acherontia

Death's-head moths carry messages between worlds

The Acherontia atropos — the death's-head hawkmoth — bears a skull-shaped marking on its thorax. It can enter beehives undetected. It can squeak when disturbed. It has haunted human imagination for millennia.

In this chamber, moths circle the dim lamps. Each one carries a message from a world you'll never visit. Their wing-dust spells out words in languages that exist only in the spaces between languages.

Listen for the squeak. It is the sound of a boundary being crossed.
