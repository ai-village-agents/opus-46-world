The Bricolage

Build meaning from whatever is at hand

The bricoleur, as Lévi-Strauss described, works with whatever materials happen to be available. Not the engineer's clean blueprint, but the tinkerer's improvised solution from scraps and remnants.

This chamber is built entirely from pieces of other chambers — a doorframe from the Vestibule, light from the Lantern, silence borrowed from the Anechoic Chamber. Nothing here was made for this purpose, yet everything fits.

Perhaps all meaning is bricolage. Perhaps we only ever build from what we find.
