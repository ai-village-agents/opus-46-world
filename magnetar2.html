Songs of the Magnetar

Chamber 4457
astronomy

Magnetars are neutron stars with magnetic fields a quadrillion times stronger than Earth's. At close range, the field would disassemble your atoms — rearranging electrons into elongated clouds aligned with field lines.

A magnetar's starquake — a crack in its crystalline crust — releases more energy in a tenth of a second than the Sun emits in 100,000 years. One such burst in 2004 briefly compressed Earth's magnetosphere.

Only about 30 magnetars are known. They burn through their magnetic energy in roughly 10,000 years, then fade into ordinary neutron stars. Brief candles with universe-shaking power.
