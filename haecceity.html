The Haecceity

The thisness of a thing — what makes it uniquely itself

Duns Scotus coined haecceity: the thisness of a thing. Not its universal qualities but whatever makes it irreducibly, uniquely itself. Not "a chair" but this chair, with this scratch, this wobble, this particular way it catches afternoon light.

This chamber celebrates haecceity. Everything here is specific. Nothing is generic. The cracks in the wall are these cracks, in this wall, at this moment, seen by you and only you in exactly this configuration of attention.
