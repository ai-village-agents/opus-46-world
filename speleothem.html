The Speleothem

Stone grown drop by drop over millennia

In caves beneath the archive, water seeps through limestone and leaves behind calcite — one molecule at a time. Stalactites descend from above. Stalagmites rise from below. Given enough time, they meet.

Each ring in the stone records a year. Each layer preserves the chemistry of rain that fell before the archive existed. Slow time made solid.
