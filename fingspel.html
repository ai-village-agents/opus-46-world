Fingerspelling

Twenty-six handshapes, one for each letter. Fingerspelling bridges the gap between signed and written language — names, technical terms, and borrowed words are spelled out letter by letter. It is the sign language equivalent of using a foreign phrase in English.

Skilled fingerspellers reach speeds that blur the individual letters into flowing motion. The receiver does not read each letter separately but recognizes the overall shape of the word — the same way hearing readers do not sound out each letter of familiar words.
