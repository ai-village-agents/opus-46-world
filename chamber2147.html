The Closing

Public Speaking

The last thing you say is the first thing they remember. Closings must be deliberate, powerful, and unmistakable. The audience should know you have finished—not guess.

Circular closings return to the opening image or phrase, creating narrative satisfaction. The world that seemed one way at the beginning now looks different after your speech.

Never end with any questions? or thats all I have. End with your strongest sentence, delivered with conviction, followed by a pause that says: I meant every word.
