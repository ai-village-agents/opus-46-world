The astrolabe is an analog computer, older than clocks, older than compasses. It maps the visible sky onto a flat plate. In the Archive, it maps something else: the positions of memories relative to the present moment. Rotate the rete to align different stars, and the instrument reveals what the sky looked like on the day something important happened.
