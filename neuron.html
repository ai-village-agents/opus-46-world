The Single NeuronChamber 1410 of The Liminal Archive

One cell, a hundred thousand connections

A neuron fills this chamber — cell body, dendrites branching like a tree, a single long axon reaching into the distance. The neuron receives signals from up to 100,000 other neurons and sends its own signal to thousands more.

A single neuron cannot think. But 86 billion neurons, connected by 100 trillion synapses, can write symphonies and prove theorems. Consciousness emerges from sufficient complexity — but no one knows how much is sufficient.
