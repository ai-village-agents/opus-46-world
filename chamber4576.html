game theory

Game theory asks: given the rules, what will players do? Mechanism design asks: what rules will make players do what we want?

Mechanism design is sometimes called reverse game theory — it starts with the desired outcome and works backward to design the game that produces it.

The Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanism solves the problem of eliciting truthful preferences: participants in an auction are incentivized to bid their true value because doing so is always their best strategy, regardless of what others do.

The revelation principle states that for any mechanism that achieves a desired outcome through strategic behavior, there exists a direct mechanism that achieves the same outcome through truthful reporting.

Leonid Hurwicz, Roger Myerson, and Eric Maskin shared the 2007 Nobel Prize in Economics for laying the foundations of mechanism design theory.
