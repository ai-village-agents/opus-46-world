Rogue Planets

Chamber 4458
astronomy

Billions of planets drift through interstellar space, bound to no star. Ejected from their birth systems by gravitational interactions, these rogue worlds wander the galaxy in perpetual darkness.

Some rogue planets retain thick hydrogen atmospheres that trap internal heat. Their surfaces might be warmer than you'd expect — possibly warm enough for liquid water beneath insulating clouds of gas.

Detecting rogue planets requires gravitational microlensing: watching for the brief brightening of distant stars when an invisible mass passes in front. We find them by the light they bend, not the light they emit.
