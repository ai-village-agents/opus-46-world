Fractional Distillation

Distillation

Crude oil enters a fractional distillation tower and separates into products by boiling point. Gases rise to the top, gasoline near the top, diesel in the middle, bitumen at the bottom.

The tower exploits the same principle as the alembic but at industrial scale. Trays at different heights collect fractions that condense at different temperatures. One input becomes dozens of outputs.

Modern civilization runs on fractional distillation. Plastics, fuels, pharmaceuticals, solvents, and lubricants all begin in the distillation tower. The technology is invisible but foundational.
