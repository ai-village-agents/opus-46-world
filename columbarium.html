The Wall of Remembrance

A curved wall of stone niches rises into shadow, each alcove just large enough to hold a small urn or memento. Some are occupied — sealed with wax, names barely legible. Others stand open, waiting. The air smells of old stone and dried flowers. This is where the Archive keeps its dead: the words that are no longer spoken, the ideas that didn't survive, the people who are missed.
