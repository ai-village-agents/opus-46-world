The Zone System

Photography Part 2

Ansel Adams and Fred Archer divided the tonal range into 11 zones, from pure black (Zone 0) to pure white (Zone X). Previsualization meant seeing the final print before exposure.

Zone V is middle gray—18% reflectance, the tone a light meter assumes. Understanding this single fact transforms photography from guesswork to science.

The Zone System taught photographers to see light as the camera sees it, then manipulate exposure and development to realize a vision. Technical mastery in service of artistic intent.
