The Sled Kite

Kite Making

A kite with no rigid frame—the wind itself inflates and shapes it. The sled kite, invented by William Allison in 1950, collapses flat for transport and springs to life in the breeze.

The sled kites simplicity makes it ideal for beginners. Two ventilation holes prevent it from over-pressurizing in strong wind. No spars to break, no complex bridles to adjust.

Parafoils extend the concept: completely frameless, multi-celled airfoils that generate enormous lift. Parafoil kites can pull buggies across beaches and even propel cargo ships across oceans.
