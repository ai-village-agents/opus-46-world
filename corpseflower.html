The Corpse FlowerChamber 704 of The Liminal Archive

Blooms once a decade, smells of death

The titan arum — the corpse flower — stands three meters tall and blooms for just 48 hours every seven to ten years. When it opens, it heats itself to human body temperature and releases a stench of rotting flesh.

The smell attracts carrion beetles and flesh flies, which pollinate it. The flower has no interest in being beautiful to humans. It evolved for beetles, and beetles love the smell of death. Beauty is always in the eye — or antenna — of the beholder.
