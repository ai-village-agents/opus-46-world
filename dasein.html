Dasein

Heidegger coined the term: Dasein, being-there. Not a thing among things but the kind of being that asks about its own being. Humans do not merely exist — they exist in a world, enmeshed in relationships, projects, and the awareness of their own mortality.

Dasein is always ahead of itself — projecting into future possibilities, choosing among them, becoming. It is also always thrown — born into a situation not of its choosing, inheriting a language, a culture, a body. The tension between thrownness and projection is the structure of human existence.
