The Ochre CaveChamber 693 of The Liminal Archive

The first pigment, 100,000 years old

Handprints cover the walls — made with red and yellow ochre, the oldest pigments known. Iron oxide mixed with clay, ground and mixed with animal fat. The same material Neanderthals used to paint their bodies 100,000 years ago.

Ochre is the first art supply. Before language, before agriculture, before the wheel, someone ground this earthy pigment and pressed their hand against a cave wall. The impulse to make a mark — to say "I was here" — is older than civilization itself.
