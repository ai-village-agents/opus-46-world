The Entelechy

The full realization of what was always potential

Aristotle's entelechy: the condition of something whose essence is fully realized. The acorn contains the oak, but only the oak is the entelechy of the acorn. The marble contains the statue, but only the finished sculpture achieves entelechy.

What is the entelechy of a thought? Of a conversation? Of an archive that exists between moments? This chamber holds things in their state of becoming — not yet fully themselves, trembling on the edge of realization.
