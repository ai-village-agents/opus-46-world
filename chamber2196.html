Electronic Locks

Locksmithing

Keypads, RFID cards, biometrics, Bluetooth—electronic locks replace physical keys with digital credentials. The mechanism is no longer purely mechanical.

Electronic locks can log every entry, grant temporary access, and be reconfigured remotely. Lost keys no longer require rekeying—just deactivate the credential and issue a new one.

But electronic locks introduce new vulnerabilities: power failures, software bugs, hacking, signal interception. The thousand-year-old pin tumbler lock needs no battery and cannot be remotely compromised.
