The weight of thinking

Working memory holds roughly seven items. Try to hold eight and something drops. Try to solve a complex problem while remembering a phone number and watch both tasks degrade. The mind has bandwidth, and it is narrower than we imagine.

Cognitive load explains why distracted driving kills, why multitasking is a myth, why simple instructions work better than complex ones. The brain is powerful but has strict throughput limits that no amount of willpower can expand.

Every interface, every instruction manual, every classroom lesson is a negotiation with cognitive load. Make it too complex and nothing gets through. Make it too simple and nothing gets learned. The art is in the balance.
