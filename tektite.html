The Tektite

Glass born from celestial impact — the sky kissing the earth

When a meteorite strikes the earth with sufficient violence, it melts the rock beneath into glass that is flung skyward, cooling into aerodynamic teardrops as it falls. These tektites are hybrids — part terrestrial, part celestial, forged in the instant of impact.

Hold a tektite and you hold a moment of transformation: the exact instant when something from beyond became part of the earth, and something of the earth was launched toward the sky. Neither purely one thing nor the other.
