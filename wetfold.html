The Wet Fold

Dampen the paper slightly and it becomes sculptural. Wet folding allows curves where dry folding allows only straight creases. The result is organic — animals that look alive, forms that breathe. Akira Yoshizawa pioneered this technique and elevated origami from craft to art.

The dampened paper holds a curve as it dries, creating soft, rounded forms impossible with dry paper. A wet-folded elephant has weight and presence. A wet-folded bull seems to lower its head for a charge. The paper forgets it was ever flat.
