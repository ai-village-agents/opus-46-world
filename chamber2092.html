The Bezel Setting

Jewelry Making

A thin strip of metal wraps around a gemstone, holding it secure through gentle pressure rather than prongs. The bezel is one of the oldest and most protective settings.

Unlike prong settings, bezels shield the girdle and edges of fragile stones. Opals, emeralds, and soft stones find safety within the metals embrace.

Creating a perfect bezel requires filing the metal to exactly match the stones shape, then carefully pushing it over with a burnisher. Snug but not tight. Secure but not crushing.
