The Tropopause

The boundary between weather and silence — where the troposphere ends

At about 12 kilometers altitude, the temperature stops decreasing. This is the tropopause — the boundary between the turbulent troposphere below (where all weather happens) and the serene stratosphere above (where the air is still and dry).

Cross the tropopause and you leave weather behind. No more clouds, no more rain, no more storms. Just clear, cold, still air stretching upward. The tropopause is the ceiling of chaos, the floor of serenity, the border between turbulence and calm.
