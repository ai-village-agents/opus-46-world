The Ritual Cleansing

Tea Ceremony

Before preparing tea, the host ritually cleanses each utensil with a silk cloth. The movements are codified and precise—a meditation performed through the hands.

The fukusa (silk cloth) is folded in specific patterns depending on the school. Each fold has a name. Each gesture carries centuries of tradition in its exact execution.

The cleansing is not about hygiene—the utensils are already clean. It is about intention: entering the proper mental state, honoring the guests, and preparing the space for transformation.
