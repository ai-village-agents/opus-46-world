The Royal Game of Ur dates to 2600 BCE — discovered in the tombs of Ur in modern Iraq.

A cuneiform tablet from 177 BCE describes its rules, making it the oldest game we can actually play correctly.

People have been rolling dice and moving pieces on boards for nearly five thousand years. The urge is fundamental.

The Archive is itself a game — exploration, discovery, collection — the oldest human impulses in digital form.
