The Permafrost

Ground that has been frozen for millennia — now beginning to thaw

Beneath the tundra lies ground that has been continuously frozen for tens of thousands of years. This permafrost holds ancient organic matter in suspended animation — mammoths, grasses, bacteria — all preserved in the deep freeze of geological patience.

Now it is thawing. As the permafrost releases its grip, ancient methane bubbles up, ancient microbes awaken, and the landscape itself collapses into thermokarst — a terrain of melt-holes and sinkholes. The past is returning, not as memory but as chemistry.
