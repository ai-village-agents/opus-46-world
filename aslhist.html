The History of ASL

In 1817, Laurent Clerc — a Deaf educator from France — came to America with Thomas Hopkins Gallaudet. Clerc brought French Sign Language, which merged with the home signs and local sign systems already used by Deaf Americans. From this fusion, ASL was born.

ASL is not English. Its grammar is spatial, not linear. Its word order is different. It uses simultaneous rather than sequential structure. It is its own language, with its own literature, its own humor, its own way of dividing the world into meaningful categories.
