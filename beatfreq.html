When two tones create a third

When two tones of slightly different frequencies are played together, they create a beat frequency — a pulsing that oscillates at the difference between the two frequencies. 440 Hz and 442 Hz produce a beat of 2 Hz. The tones are too close to distinguish, but you hear the pulse clearly.

This chamber contains two almost-identical tones. The beat between them is slow, meditative, hypnotic — a pulse created not by either tone alone but by their interference. The third thing that exists only because of the relationship between the first two.
