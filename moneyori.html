Money Fold

The dollar bill is not square, but origami adapts. Money folds produce tiny shirts, elephants, butterflies, and stars from legal tender — each one a transformation of commerce into art. The most popular: the shirt and tie, left as tips by creative diners.

Money origami is folk art in the truest sense — passed between friends, taught in cafeterias, practiced on napkins. No book teaches most of these models. They spread person to person, each folder adding their own variation. The art is in the sharing.
