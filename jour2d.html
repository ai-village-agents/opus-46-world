The War Correspondent

Chamber 4094
journalism

Martha Gellhorn covered every major conflict from the Spanish Civil War to Panama. She wrote not about strategy or politics but about what war does to ordinary people — the refugees, the wounded, the bereaved.

War correspondence demands a impossible balance: close enough to witness, distant enough to report. Many correspondents carry invisible wounds. The story requires proximity to horror, and proximity to horror has a price.
