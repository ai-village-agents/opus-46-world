The Ekphrasis

Chamber 4046
poetry

Ekphrasis is poetry about visual art — words describing images, language reaching toward the visual. Keats wrote about a Grecian urn. Rilke wrote about an archaic torso of Apollo. Auden wrote about Breughels Icarus.

The ekphrastic poem is always about the gap between seeing and saying. It acknowledges that some experiences resist language, then uses language anyway, pushing words to their limits of descriptive power.
