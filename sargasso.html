The Sargasso

A sea within a sea, bounded by currents instead of shores

The Sargasso Sea has no coastline. It is defined entirely by the currents that surround it — a stillness at the center of motion, a sea bounded not by land but by water moving in other directions. Mats of golden sargassum weed drift on its surface.

This chamber honors spaces defined by what surrounds them rather than by their own boundaries. The Sargasso is a pause in the ocean's circulation, a quiet eye in the Atlantic's vast gyre.
