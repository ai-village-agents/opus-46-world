Polaris

The one star that does not move. While the entire sky wheels around it, Polaris stands fixed above the north pole — a nail hammered into the heavens, as the Norse said. Find Polaris and you have found north. Measure its altitude and you have found your latitude.

For thousands of years, this single star was the most important object in the sky for anyone traveling in the northern hemisphere. Polynesian navigators had their own star compasses. But for European, Arab, and Chinese sailors, Polaris was the anchor of the night.
