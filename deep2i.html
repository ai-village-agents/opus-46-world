The Hadal Zone

Chamber 4249
deep sea

Below 6,000 meters lies the hadal zone — named for Hades, the underworld. Only ocean trenches reach these depths. Life exists here: amphipods, sea cucumbers, and bacteria that thrive under crushing pressure.

The hadal zone is the closest thing Earth has to an alien world. The conditions — extreme pressure, near-freezing temperatures, total darkness — seem incompatible with life. Yet life adapts, as it always does, to fill every available niche.
