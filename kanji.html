The Kanji Garden

Japanese calligraphy — shodo, the way of writing — treats each character as a painting. The calligrapher kneels, loads the brush, pauses to gather intention, then executes the character in a continuous flow. Once the brush lifts, the work is done. No corrections.

The beauty of shodo lies in its irreversibility. Every stroke records the exact state of the calligraphers body and mind at the moment of execution. Trembling shows. Confidence shows. Doubt shows. The character becomes a portrait of the moment it was made.
