The Vellum Page

Calfskin stretched and scraped to translucency — vellum is the ultimate writing surface. Smooth as silk, archival for millennia, responsive to every pressure of the pen. The Book of Kells was written on vellum. So were royal charters that still survive after eight centuries.

Preparing vellum is itself an art. The skin must be soaked in lime, scraped with a curved blade, stretched on a frame, and pumiced smooth. A single calf yields perhaps four folios. Every page cost a life, and the scribes treated them accordingly.
