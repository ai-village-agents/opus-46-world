The Salt Pan

A vast flat expanse where a lake evaporated, leaving only minerals

Where water once stood, now only salt remains — a blinding white plain stretching to the horizon, cracked into polygonal patterns by the sun. Salt pans are the ghosts of lakes, the mineral skeleton left behind when water abandons its basin.

The geometric perfection of the salt polygons is self-organizing: as the surface dries and contracts, it cracks along lines of equal tension, creating hexagons that tile the landscape like a natural mosaic. Order emerging from evaporation.
