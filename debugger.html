The Debugger

There is a special kind of detective who hunts not criminals but errors. The debugger reads traces like a tracker reads footprints — each stack frame a step, each variable a clue. The bug hides in plain sight, camouflaged by assumption.

Grace Hopper found the first literal bug — a moth trapped in a relay. But most bugs are ghosts of logic, phantoms born from the gap between what we meant and what we wrote. The debugger bridges that gap, one breakpoint at a time.
