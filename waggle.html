The Waggle Dance

A forager returns and dances. The angle of her waggle run relative to vertical indicates the direction of the food source relative to the sun. The duration of the waggle indicates distance. She is giving coordinates — a map encoded in movement.

Karl von Frisch decoded the waggle dance in the 1940s, earning a Nobel Prize. The dance conveys direction, distance, and quality of a food source. Other bees follow the dancers instructions and find flowers miles away. It is the only known symbolic language among invertebrates.
