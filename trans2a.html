The Roman Road

Chamber 4281
transportation

The Romans built 80,000 kilometers of roads across their empire — engineered with multiple layers of gravel and stone, crowned for drainage, and built to last. Some are still in use two thousand years later.

Roman roads did not just move armies. They moved ideas, goods, and culture. The road network unified an empire. When Rome fell, the roads remained — and the medieval world grew along the routes Rome had drawn.
