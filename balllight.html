Ball LightningChamber 582 of The Liminal Archive

The phenomenon that physics cannot explain

Ball lightning: luminous spheres that appear during thunderstorms, drifting through the air, sometimes passing through walls or windows. Observed for centuries, reported by thousands of witnesses, yet no scientific theory fully explains them. They last seconds to minutes, then vanish — sometimes silently, sometimes with an explosion.

A single sphere of ball lightning hovers in this chamber. It pulses, drifts, changes color. It should not exist. It does anyway.
