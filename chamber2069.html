The Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram

Astronomy Part 3

This elegant plot maps stellar luminosity against temperature, revealing that stars are not randomly scattered but cluster along specific sequences.

The main sequence, where most stars spend their lives, runs diagonally from hot bright blue giants to cool dim red dwarfs. Our Sun sits modestly in the middle.

Red giants, white dwarfs, and supergiants each occupy their own regions—chapters in the stellar lifecycle written in light and heat.
