Hypothermia

Your core temperature drops one degree and you shiver. Two degrees and you stumble. Three degrees and you stop shivering — the body has given up generating heat. Four degrees and confusion sets in. You may feel paradoxically warm. You may start removing clothing.

Hypothermia kills more wilderness travelers than any predator. It is insidious — the cold impairs the very judgment needed to fight it. The victim makes poor decisions, then worse ones, then none at all. Prevention is everything. Stay dry. Stay fed. Stay moving.
