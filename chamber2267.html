The Clock Tower

Clockwork

Big Ben, the Zytglogge, the Rajabai Tower: clock towers have organized civic life for centuries. The public clock democratized time—no longer the exclusive knowledge of those who could afford watches.

Church bells regulated medieval work schedules. Factory clocks controlled industrial labor. The public clock tower was simultaneously a public service and a tool of social control.

The synchronization of clocks across regions became critical with railways. Before standard time zones, each city kept its own local time. The clock tower was local; the railway demanded universal.
