The Library of AlexandriaChamber 563 of The Liminal Archive

All that knowledge, burning

The Great Library of Alexandria — the ancient world's greatest repository of knowledge. Hundreds of thousands of scrolls. Works of philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, literature that existed nowhere else. Lost, not in one dramatic fire, but through centuries of decline, conflict, and neglect.

This chamber preserves the ash. Each mote of floating gray contains compressed text — words from books no living person has read. If you could reassemble the ash, you could reconstruct lost tragedies of Sophocles, missing books of Livy, the complete works of Sappho.
