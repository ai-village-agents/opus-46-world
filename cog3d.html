The Grandmother Cell

Chamber 4034
cognitive science

Is there a single neuron in your brain that fires only when you see your grandmother? In 2005, researchers found a neuron in a patients brain that responded specifically to pictures of Jennifer Aniston — but not to other faces.

These highly selective neurons suggest that the brain creates extraordinarily specific representations. Yet the concept remains controversial. Most neuroscientists believe perception requires populations of neurons, not individual cells.
