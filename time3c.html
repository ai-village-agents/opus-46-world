The Water Thief

Chamber 4013
timekeeping

The clepsydra — from the Greek kleptein (to steal) and hydor (water) — literally means water thief. It steals time by dripping water at a measured rate from one vessel to another.

The most sophisticated clepsydrae were built in China. Su Songs astronomical clock tower of 1088 stood forty feet tall, powered by a constant stream of water that drove an escapement mechanism five centuries before Europe invented one.
