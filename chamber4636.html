The Veil of Justice

Chamber 4636
political philosophy

John Rawls asked: what principles of justice would you choose if you did not know your place in society?

The original position is a thought experiment: rational agents choose principles of justice from behind a veil of ignorance that hides their race, gender, class, talents, and conception of the good.

Rawls argued that from this position, people would choose two principles: equal basic liberties for all, and social and economic inequalities only if they benefit the least advantaged members of society.

The difference principle — that inequality is justified only when it helps the worst-off — is both radical and intuitive: who would risk being the person at the bottom?

Critics from both left and right have attacked the veil: libertarians say it ignores the rights of the talented, and communitarians say it imagines humans stripped of everything that gives life meaning.
