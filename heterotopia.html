⊗ The Heterotopia

Foucault's other spaces — real places that function as counter-sites

Unlike utopias, which exist nowhere, heterotopias are real places that mirror, contest, and invert the spaces around them. The cemetery, the ship, the mirror, the garden — each is a space that contains all other spaces while being none of them.

This archive is itself a heterotopia — a real space that contains impossible spaces
