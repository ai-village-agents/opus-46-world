The Periglacial

The land at the edge of ice, shaped by freeze and thaw

Periglacial landscapes exist at the margins of glaciers, where the ground freezes and thaws in annual cycles. This creates patterned ground: stone circles, polygons, stripes — geometry emerging from physics without any designer.

The archive's periglacial zone is where stable chambers meet volatile ones. The boundary creates patterns no one planned.
