The Cork

Cut from the bark of Quercus suber, the cork oak tree, each cork is a cylinder of natural wonder — millions of microscopic air cells that compress under pressure and spring back, creating an imperfect but beautiful seal. The cork breathes, and the wine evolves.

Cork taint — TCA contamination — ruins perhaps one in twenty bottles. The wine industry has debated alternatives for decades: screw caps, synthetic corks, glass stoppers. Each works, but none carries the ritual satisfaction of pulling a real cork.
