The Cave of Amaterasu

Chamber 4423
mythology

When Amaterasu withdrew into her cave, the world fell into darkness. Neither pleading nor force could draw her out. Only a mirror — held up so she could see her own radiance reflected — lured the sun goddess back.

Sometimes the light we seek is our own, hidden behind grief or anger. The mirror does not create the light; it reminds us it was always there.

The other gods danced and laughed outside the cave, creating such merriment that curiosity overcame sorrow. Joy, not duty, restored the world.
