The Sublime

Not beauty but something beyond beauty — the sublime is the feeling evoked by vastness, power, or infinity that exceeds our capacity to comprehend. Standing at the edge of a canyon. Watching a storm at sea. Contemplating the number of stars. The mind reaches and fails. That failure is the sublime.

Burke distinguished the beautiful (smooth, small, delicate) from the sublime (vast, powerful, terrifying). Kant went further: the sublime reveals the limitations of our senses while affirming the power of our reason. We cannot see the infinite, but we can think it. The gap between seeing and thinking is the sublime.
