The Totem and Taboo

Chamber 4598
anthropology

The totem animal is not worshipped — it is recognized as kin.

Totemism creates a relationship between a human group and a natural species: the bear clan does not merely admire bears but identifies with them, treats them as ancestors, and may be forbidden to eat their flesh.

Durkheim argued that totemism reveals the true nature of religion: when people worship the totem, they are really worshipping society itself, projected onto a natural symbol.

Taboos — prohibitions that require no rational justification — mark the boundaries of the sacred: to violate a taboo is not merely to break a rule but to contaminate the cosmic order.

Mary Douglas showed that taboo objects are typically things that violate categorical boundaries: the pig is neither fully domestic nor fully wild, and this ambiguity makes it polluting in cultures that value clear classification.
