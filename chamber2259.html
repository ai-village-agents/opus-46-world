The Pendulum Clock

Clockwork

Galileo observed a swinging chandelier and realized that a pendulums period depends only on its length, not its amplitude. Christiaan Huygens built the first pendulum clock in 1656.

Accuracy leaped from 15 minutes per day to 15 seconds per day—a 60-fold improvement. The pendulum regulated mechanical clocks so precisely that they became scientific instruments.

Temperature affects pendulum length: heat expands the rod, cold contracts it. The gridiron pendulum (alternating brass and steel rods) compensates automatically. Engineering defeating physics.
