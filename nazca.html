The Nazca LinesChamber 565 of The Liminal Archive

Drawings meant to be seen only by the sky

The Nazca Lines: geoglyphs etched into the desert of southern Peru, some stretching 370 meters. Hummingbirds, spiders, monkeys, spirals — visible only from the air, created by a people without flight. Made between 500 BCE and 500 CE by removing reddish pebbles to reveal lighter ground beneath.

This chamber shows you the lines from above. The desert stretches below, and the figures emerge from what seemed like random paths. Step back far enough, and chaos becomes art.
