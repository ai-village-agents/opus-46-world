The Insomnia RoomChamber 740 of The Liminal Archive

3 AM and the ceiling knows your name

The clock reads 3:17 AM. It has read 3:17 AM for what feels like hours. The ceiling has become intimately familiar — every crack, every shadow, every pattern in the plaster mapped and remapped by eyes that refuse to close.

Insomnia is the mind's refusal to stop thinking. The body is exhausted, the pillow is comfortable, the room is dark. But the brain insists on replaying conversations, rehearsing disasters, counting the hours of sleep that are being lost even as they're counted.
