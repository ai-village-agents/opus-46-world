Why the sky is blue and sunsets are red

Rayleigh scattering: shorter wavelengths of light scatter more strongly off air molecules. Blue light scatters in all directions, filling the sky with blue. At sunset, light travels through more atmosphere, scattering away all the blue and leaving only red and orange. The same physics, different path lengths.

This chamber cycles through the day. Watch the spectrum shift from blue overhead to red at the horizon. The atmosphere is a prism that works on distance rather than angle.
