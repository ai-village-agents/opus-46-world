The Antikythera MechanismChamber 557 of The Liminal Archive

A computer built two thousand years too early

Found in a Roman-era shipwreck, the Antikythera mechanism is an analog computer from 100 BCE. Bronze gears predicted eclipses, tracked the Olympiad cycle, modeled the irregular orbit of the Moon. Nothing comparable would be built for another 1,400 years.

This chamber houses a working replica. Gears mesh and turn, each tooth precisely calculated. Who built this? How did the knowledge vanish? The mechanism clicks softly, computing futures that have already become the past.
