The Thermohaline Circulation

The great ocean conveyor belt that takes a thousand years to complete one loop

Driven by differences in temperature and salinity, the thermohaline circulation is a global river within the ocean. Cold, salty water sinks in the North Atlantic, flows along the ocean floor to the Pacific, rises, warms, and returns — a journey that takes roughly 1,000 years.

The archive has its own thermohaline circulation. Old ideas sink, travel through deep chambers, and resurface transformed in distant parts of the map.

The great conveyor belt circles the globe.
