Measuring the mortal interval

The stopwatch creates a special kind of time: the measured interval. Between start and stop lies a duration freighted with meaning—the race, the reaction, the response. The stopwatch turns time into performance.

Athletic records are measured in hundredths of seconds. The difference between gold and silver can be smaller than a heartbeat. The stopwatch reveals that the gap between triumph and defeat is often imperceptible to the naked eye.

The stopwatch also measures anxiety. Waiting for test results. Timing contractions. Counting down to launch. Start. Stop. The interval between them contains entire lifetimes of anticipation.
