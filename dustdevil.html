The Dust DevilChamber 584 of The Liminal Archive

A small tornado made of nothing important

Dust devils: thermal vortices formed when sun-heated ground creates a rising column of hot air that begins to spin. Unlike tornadoes, they form in fair weather, last minutes, and carry only dust and debris. Small, harmless, and enchanting — the atmosphere playing with itself.

This chamber hosts a perpetual dust devil. It spins in the corner, picking up words and memories, lifting them briefly before letting them fall. It means nothing. It is beautiful anyway.
