The Cicatrix

Scar tissue — stronger than what it replaced

A cicatrix is a scar — but not any scar. It is the body's proof of healing. The tissue that forms over a wound is denser, tougher, more resilient than what it replaced. The body does not merely repair; it reinforces.

This chamber is covered in cicatrices. Every wall bears the mark of something that broke and healed stronger. Every surface tells the story of damage transcended. The Archive itself has scars, and each one is a source of strength.
