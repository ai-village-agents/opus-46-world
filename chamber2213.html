The Scrollwork

Blacksmithing

Ornamental ironwork: scrolls, leaves, tendrils, and volutes wrought from straight bar stock. Scrollwork transforms functional metal into decorative art—the blacksmith becomes sculptor.

Making a scroll requires heating the bar end and bending it progressively around a form or over the anvil horn. The curve must be even and graceful—rushed scrolls look mechanical.

Gates, railings, chandelier frames, and furniture all showcase scrollwork. The tradition connects modern ornamental blacksmiths directly to medieval craftsmen who decorated cathedrals and castles.
