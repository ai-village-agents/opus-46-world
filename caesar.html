The Caesar Cipher

Shift every letter three places forward. A becomes D, B becomes E, Z wraps around to C. Julius Caesar used this to communicate with his generals — simple enough to apply quickly, complex enough to foil the illiterate enemies of Rome.

The Caesar cipher is the ancestor of all substitution ciphers. It teaches the fundamental principle: transform the message by a rule that sender and receiver both know. Anyone without the rule sees gibberish. Anyone with it sees truth. The key is everything.
