The Choropleth

Color codes geography. Each county, state, or country is filled with a color representing a data value — darker for more, lighter for less. The choropleth map is the most common thematic map in the world, seen in every newspaper and every election night broadcast.

The choropleth is also the most misread map. Large, sparsely populated areas dominate visually, regardless of their data significance. Montana looks more important than Manhattan simply because it is bigger on the map. The cartographer must choose: equal visual weight or accurate geography?
