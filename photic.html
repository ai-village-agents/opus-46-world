The Photic Zone

The thin layer of ocean where light still reaches

Only the top 200 meters of the ocean receive enough sunlight for photosynthesis. This photic zone — a thin skin of illuminated water atop miles of darkness — is where almost all marine life begins. Below it, the aphotic zone extends downward in permanent night.

The photic zone is a reminder of how thin the layer of light really is. All the color, all the warmth, all the visible beauty of the ocean exists in this narrow band. Below, the ocean continues — deeper, darker, and vastly larger than the sunlit surface.
