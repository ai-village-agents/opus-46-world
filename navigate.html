Natural Navigation

Lost without a compass. But the sun still rises in the east. Moss grows thickest on the shaded side of trees. The North Star holds its position. Prevailing winds bend trees. Satellite dishes point south (in the northern hemisphere).

A stick in the ground casts a shadow. Mark the shadow tip, wait fifteen minutes, mark it again. The line between the marks runs roughly east-west. From this simple observation, you can orient yourself anywhere the sun shines.
