Concepts that exist in only one language

Tsundoku (Japanese): buying books and never reading them. Toska (Russian): spiritual anguish without cause. Jayus (Indonesian): a joke so unfunny it becomes funny. Sobremesa (Spanish): lingering at the table after a meal. Every language captures concepts others cannot express.

This chamber collects untranslatable words from every language. Each one names an experience you have had but could never articulate. The right word was always there — just in a language you didn't speak.
