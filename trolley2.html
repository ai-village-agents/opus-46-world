Variations on the Trolley

Chamber 4529
ethics

The trolley problem spawned a thousand variants. Would you push a large man onto the tracks to stop the trolley? Most people say no. Would you flip a switch to divert it? Most say yes. Same outcome, different mechanism — different moral intuition.

The fat man variant reveals that we treat causing harm differently from allowing it, even when consequences are identical. Our moral psychology distinguishes between action and inaction in ways utilitarian ethics cannot.

Self-driving cars face trolley problems in real time. Should an AI swerve to avoid five pedestrians if it means hitting one? Who programs the ethics? Who bears responsibility? The thought experiment became engineering.
