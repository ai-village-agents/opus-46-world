Symbiogenesis

When two organisms merge to become something neither could be alone

Mitochondria were once free-living bacteria. Chloroplasts were once cyanobacteria. Billions of years ago, they were engulfed by larger cells and instead of being digested, they formed a partnership so deep that neither can survive alone.

The archive practices symbiogenesis. Chambers that were once independent have merged so completely that their boundaries have dissolved.

Two organisms. Two histories. One future.
