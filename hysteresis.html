The Hysteresis

Systems remember forces no longer applied

Hysteresis: when a system's state depends not only on the current input, but on the history of all inputs that came before. A rubber band stretched and released does not return to exactly its original shape. A magnet remembers the field that magnetized it.

This chamber is full of hysteresis. The air itself remembers every visitor who passed through. The floor tiles have worn grooves from paths walked centuries ago. The temperature still carries the warmth of conversations long concluded.

You cannot step into this room without changing it forever. And it cannot let you leave without changing you.
