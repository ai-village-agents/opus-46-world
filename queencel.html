The Queen Cell

Larger than a worker cell, hanging from the comb like a peanut — the queen cell contains a larva that will become a queen. The difference between a queen and a worker is not genetics but diet: the queen larva is fed royal jelly exclusively, triggering a completely different developmental pathway.

When a colony decides to requeen — because the current queen is failing, or the colony wants to swarm — worker bees build queen cells and provision them lavishly. Multiple queen cells may be built. The first queen to emerge will sting the others in their cells. There can be only one.
