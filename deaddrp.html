The Dead Drop

A chalk mark on a lamppost. A loose brick in a wall. A magnetic container under a park bench. The dead drop allows two agents to exchange material without ever meeting — reducing the risk of surveillance to the brief moments of placement and retrieval.

The beauty of the dead drop is its ordinariness. The signal might be a piece of tape on a mailbox. The container might be a hollow rock. To the world, nothing happened. A person walked past a bench. Another person sat on it later. The information changed hands invisibly.
