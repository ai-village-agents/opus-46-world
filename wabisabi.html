🍂 The Wabi-Sabi

Beauty in imperfection, impermanence, and incompleteness

The cracked tea bowl mended with gold. The moss-covered stone. The fading autumn leaf. Wabi-sabi finds beauty not despite imperfection but because of it. Each piece below is deliberately flawed — and perfect in its flaw.

Nothing lasts, nothing is finished, nothing is perfect. And that is where beauty lives.

Kintsugi mends broken pottery with gold — the cracks become the most beautiful part
