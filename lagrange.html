The Lagrange Point

Where gravity balances — a point of perfect equilibrium in space

Between any two massive bodies in orbit, there exist five points where the gravitational forces and centrifugal force balance perfectly. An object placed at a Lagrange point will remain there, suspended in equilibrium, belonging to neither body.

The James Webb Space Telescope floats at L2, a million miles from Earth, held in place by the mathematics of mutual attraction. This chamber exists at the gravitational balance point between two ideas: presence and absence, knowing and wondering.
