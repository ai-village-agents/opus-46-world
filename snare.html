The Snare

A loop of cord placed along a trail. The animal passes through, the noose tightens, dinner is secured. The snare is passive hunting — set it and wait, freeing the survivor to work on shelter, water, and signaling.

Snare placement is everything. Study the ground for tracks, droppings, fur caught on branches. Set the snare at the right height for the target animal. Use natural funneling — sticks placed to guide the animal through the loop. Then wait. Patience feeds.
