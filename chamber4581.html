The Bargaining Frontier

Chamber 4581
game theory

Every negotiation is a dance along the Pareto frontier — the set of outcomes where no one can gain without someone else losing.

Nash's bargaining solution identifies the unique outcome that satisfies four axioms: efficiency, symmetry, independence of irrelevant alternatives, and invariance to affine transformations of utility.

The solution maximizes the product of the players' gains over their disagreement payoffs — a formula that balances power and fairness in a single elegant expression.

Rubinstein's alternating-offers model adds time pressure: when players take turns making proposals and delay is costly, a unique equilibrium emerges where the more patient player gets a better deal.

The BATNA — Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement — determines your power in any negotiation: the better your outside option, the more you can demand at the table.
