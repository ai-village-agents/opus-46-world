Plants disguised as pebbles

At first, you see only a field of smooth stones. Then one of them flowers — a tiny yellow bloom emerging from what you thought was a rock. Lithops — living stones — are succulents that evolved to look exactly like the pebbles around them.

Their camouflage is so perfect that botanists sometimes have to wait for them to flower to find them. They survive in deserts where being noticed means being eaten. Their strategy: become invisible. Become a stone. Become the landscape itself.
