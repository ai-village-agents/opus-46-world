The Taxidermy Studio

The animal is gone. What remains is a collaboration between art and anatomy — skin stretched over a sculpted form, eyes replaced with glass, every feather and hair positioned to suggest life. Taxidermy preserves the appearance of the living from the reality of the dead.

The word means arranging skin — from the Greek taxis (arrangement) and derma (skin). The best taxidermists are also naturalists, sculptors, and painters. They study living animals obsessively because their job is to recreate the subtle asymmetries and tensions of life from inert materials.
