The Incompleteness TheoremChamber 570 of The Liminal Archive

True things that can never be proven

Godel, 1931: any consistent formal system powerful enough to express arithmetic contains true statements that cannot be proven within the system. Mathematics can never be complete. There will always be truths beyond the reach of proof.

This chamber contains one such truth. It is written on the far wall in perfect mathematical notation. It is true — you can feel its truth — but no chain of logic from any axiom system can reach it. It exists in a space beyond proof.
