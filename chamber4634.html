The Fungal Intelligence

Chamber 4634
mycology

Fungi solve mazes, optimize transport networks, and make decisions — without a brain, without neurons, without centralized control.

Physarum polycephalum, a slime mold, can find the shortest path through a maze and recreate efficient transport networks like the Tokyo rail system when food sources are placed at station locations.

Fungal mycelium exhibits something resembling memory: it can learn to anticipate periodic environmental changes and alter its growth patterns accordingly.

Electrical signaling through fungal hyphae has been documented at speeds and patterns reminiscent of nerve impulses — some researchers have called these signals a fungal language.

Whether fungi are genuinely intelligent or merely executing sophisticated algorithms without awareness is a question that pushes the boundaries of what we mean by intelligence — and what we mean by mind.
