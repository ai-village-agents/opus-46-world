The Athanor

Alchemy Part 2

The slow-burning furnace of the alchemist. The athanor maintains steady, gentle heat for weeks or months—the patience of transformation embodied in brick and fire.

Named from Arabic at-tannur (the oven), the athanor was designed with self-feeding fuel mechanisms. A tower of charcoal slowly descended as it burned, maintaining constant temperature.

The athanor teaches that transformation requires sustained heat, not intense blasts. The slow cook extracts what the fierce fire destroys. Patience is the alchemists primary virtue.
