The Karst Landscape

Where water dissolves stone and leaves a landscape of sinkholes and caves

Karst forms when slightly acidic water dissolves limestone over thousands of years. The result: sinkholes, disappearing rivers, caves with stalactites, underground lakes. A landscape that is mostly empty space — Swiss cheese made of stone.

Water dissolves stone. The land collapses inward.
