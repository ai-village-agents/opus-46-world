The Voice Leading Geometry

Chamber 4608
music theory

The movement from one chord to another is not arbitrary — it follows paths through a geometric space where proximity means smooth connection.

Voice leading — the art of moving individual melodic lines between chords — was long treated as a set of rules to be memorized.

Dmitri Tymoczko showed that voice leading can be understood geometrically: chords are points in a mathematical space, and good voice leadings are short paths between nearby points.

In this geometric view, the great progressions of tonal harmony are not conventions but consequences of proximity in chord space — the V-I cadence works partly because the dominant chord is geometrically close to the tonic.

The geometry also reveals why certain chord progressions feel smooth and others feel surprising: distance in chord space correlates with perceptual distance in the listening experience.
