The Extraction

Uncap the cells with a hot knife — the wax caps fall away revealing golden honey beneath. Place the frames in the extractor and spin. Centrifugal force pulls the honey from the cells, and it runs down the walls to pool at the bottom. Liquid gold.

The honey flows through a strainer to remove wax particles, then into jars. Raw honey — unheated, unfiltered — retains all its enzymes, pollen, and character. Each extraction captures the specific flowers the bees visited. Spring honey differs from summer honey as clearly as white wine from red.
