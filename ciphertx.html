The Cipher Room

Every message has two audiences — the intended reader and everyone else. The cipher ensures only the first can understand. From Caesar shifts to Enigma rotors to RSA encryption, the history of espionage is the history of hiding meaning in plain sight.

The cipher room hums with machines. Rotors click. Tape feeds through readers. A message that looks like random letters is actually an agents report from behind enemy lines. Without the key, it is noise. With the key, it is everything.
