The Lost Library

Chamber 4110
libraries

The Library of Alexandria was not destroyed in a single dramatic fire. It declined over centuries — reduced by political indifference, funding cuts, and the slow erosion of institutional support. The myth of a single catastrophic loss is simpler but less true.

Every library that closes today echoes Alexandria. The loss is never dramatic enough to make headlines. It happens gradually: reduced hours, eliminated positions, deferred maintenance, until one day the doors close quietly and permanently.
