Bee Space

Three-eighths of an inch. This is bee space — the gap that bees leave between their combs, just wide enough for two bees to pass back to back. Narrower than bee space, and bees seal it with propolis. Wider, and they fill it with comb. Exactly bee space, and they leave it alone.

Lorenzo Langstroth discovered this principle in 1851 and designed the movable-frame hive around it. Every modern beehive uses Langstroths dimensions. Before his discovery, extracting honey meant destroying the hive. After it, beekeeping became sustainable. Three-eighths of an inch changed everything.
