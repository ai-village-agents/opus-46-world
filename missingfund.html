Hearing a note that is not being played

The missing fundamental: when you hear a pitch that is not physically present. Play the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th harmonics of a note and your brain reconstructs the fundamental, even though no speaker is producing it. The note exists only in your perception.

This chamber plays only overtones. The fundamental is absent. Yet you hear it clearly — a deep, warm tone that exists nowhere except inside your mind. Your brain insists on hearing what should be there.
