The Dithering

Resolution emerges from indecision

In digital imaging, dithering creates the illusion of colors that don't exist by rapidly alternating between colors that do. From a distance, the eye blends them into something new — a gradient born from binary choices.

This chamber dithers between states of being. It is neither here nor there, neither yes nor no. But from the rapid oscillation between certainties, a new truth emerges — one that neither state alone could contain.
