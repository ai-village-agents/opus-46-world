The Gift Economy

Chamber 4596
anthropology

In many societies, wealth is not accumulated but given away — and the gift creates a bond more powerful than any contract.

Marcel Mauss's The Gift (1925) revealed that gift exchange is never free: every gift creates an obligation to receive and to reciprocate.

The potlatch ceremonies of the Pacific Northwest saw chiefs compete by giving away or destroying enormous quantities of wealth — status was measured not by what you possessed but by what you could afford to lose.

The Kula ring of the Trobriand Islands circulates two types of shell valuables in opposite directions around a chain of islands, creating a network of obligation and alliance spanning hundreds of miles.

Gift economies persist in modern life: birthday presents, rounds of drinks, academic citations, and open-source software all operate on the logic of reciprocal obligation.
