The Treaty Table

The table is round so that no one sits at its head. The chairs are identical so that no one appears more powerful. These details matter — in diplomacy, symbolism is substance. The shape of the table at the Paris Peace Talks was debated for months.

Every treaty is a compromise. Each signatory sacrifices something to gain something else. The art is in making each side believe they sacrificed less than they gained. The masterwork is a treaty where this is actually true.
