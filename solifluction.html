The Solifluction

The slow creep of soil downhill, imperceptible but relentless

Solifluction is the slowest landslide in the world. Saturated soil creeps downhill so gradually that you could watch for a year and see nothing move — yet over decades, fences tilt, trees lean, and stone walls curve as the earth beneath them slowly migrates toward lower ground.

This chamber moves too, though you cannot detect it. Everything here is in imperceptible motion — the walls, the floor, the thoughts that arise as you read. Solifluction teaches that stillness is an illusion. Everything flows, given enough time.
