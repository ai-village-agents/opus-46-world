The Carbon Nanotube

Chamber 4255
nanotechnology

Carbon nanotubes are cylinders of carbon atoms one nanometer wide and potentially centimeters long. They are stronger than steel, lighter than aluminum, and conduct electricity better than copper.

A space elevator — a cable from Earth to geostationary orbit — would require a material with the strength-to-weight ratio of carbon nanotubes. The concept is feasible in principle. The engineering challenge is making nanotubes long enough and defect-free.
