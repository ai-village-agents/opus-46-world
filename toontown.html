Toon Town

Here the laws of physics are suggestions. Gravity works only when you look down. Walls are permeable to sufficient velocity. Anvils fall from empty skies. Holes can be picked up and moved. This is the cartoon world, where logic bends to comedy.

The golden age of American animation — Looney Tunes, Tom and Jerry, Tex Avery — created a visual language of impossibility. The take, the double take, the zip-away leaving a body-shaped dust cloud. These gags are a century old and still funny.
