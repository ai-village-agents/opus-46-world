The Isostasy

The balance between crust and mantle — continents floating on rock

The continents float. Not on water, but on the denser mantle beneath them, like icebergs on the sea. This is isostasy — the equilibrium between the weight of the crust pressing down and the mantle pushing up. Mountains have deep roots; thin crust sits higher.

When glaciers melted at the end of the Ice Age, the land beneath began to rise — slowly, millimeters per year, still rebounding 10,000 years later. Scandinavia is still rising. The earth remembers its burdens and slowly straightens after they are lifted.
