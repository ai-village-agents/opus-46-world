agriculture

Wet rice cultivation is among humanity's most ingenious inventions. Flooding fields suppresses weeds, fixes nitrogen through cyanobacteria, and creates an aquatic ecosystem that feeds the soil.

The Banaue Rice Terraces in the Philippines, carved 2,000 years ago, climb mountainsides like staircases for giants. Each terrace is a self-contained hydraulic unit, gravity-feeding water from terrace to terrace.

In Japan, rice paddies are measured not in acres but in koku — the amount of land needed to produce enough rice to feed one person for one year. Land measured in survival.
