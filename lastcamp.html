The Last Camp

The final night before rescue. Tomorrow, civilization returns — hot food, dry clothes, a roof. But tonight, you are still out here, still surviving, still relying on everything you have learned. The fire crackles. The shelter holds. You have water and a little food.

Something has changed. The wilderness is no longer hostile — it is simply indifferent, as it always was. The hostility was your inexperience. Now, on this last night, you understand: you were never fighting the wild. You were learning to live within it.
