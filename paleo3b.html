Amber Preservation

Chamber 3899
paleontology

Tree resin trapped insects, spiders, even small vertebrates millions of years ago, preserving them in exquisite detail.

Amber specimens show individual hairs, wing venation, parasites, and even the last meal in a trapped insects gut.

Dominican amber contains specimens up to 40 million years old. Burmese amber reaches 100 million.

The Archive aspires to amber-quality preservation — knowledge encased in digital resin, every detail intact.
