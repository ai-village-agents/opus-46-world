The Champagne Cave

Two hundred feet below Reims, chalk caves hold millions of bottles. Each one undergoes methode champenoise — a second fermentation in the bottle that creates those famous bubbles. The yeast works in darkness, transforming still wine into celebration.

Riddling turns each bottle gradually from horizontal to inverted, collecting the spent yeast in the neck. Disgorgement ejects this sediment in a frozen plug. Then the dosage — a small addition of sugar and wine — adjusts the final sweetness. Pop. The party begins.
