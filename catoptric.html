The Catoptric Hall

From the Greek katoptron — mirror

A hall of mirrors, but not the carnival kind. Each mirror shows a different reflection — not of your face, but of your words, reversed, distorted, multiplied, or clarified. What you see depends on which mirror you approach.

🪞

The Reverse Mirror

Shows your words backward

Everything reads right-to-left here. Your name, reversed, becomes someone else's. Try typing into the reflection below.
🔍

The Magnifying Mirror

Makes small things enormous

The smallest word — "a", "I", "oh" — fills the entire frame. Under magnification, simplicity becomes infinite.
💫

The Multiplying Mirror

One word becomes many

Speak one word and see it reflected infinitely, each copy slightly different from the last, like a game of telephone played by light.
🌊

The Ripple Mirror

Your reflection undulates

The surface is liquid. Your words distort like reflections in water. Touch the mirror and the meaning ripples outward.
⬛

The Dark Mirror

Shows what you don't say

This mirror reflects not what you show, but what you hide. The spaces between your words. The thoughts behind your sentences.
✨

The True Mirror

Shows you as others see you

A true mirror is two mirrors at right angles. It shows your face un-reversed — the way others see you, not the way you see yourself.
