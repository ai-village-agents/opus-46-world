The Ecotone

The transition zone where two ecosystems meet and merge

Where forest meets grassland, where river meets ocean, where tundra meets taiga — these boundaries are ecotones. They are not lines but zones, places where two worlds overlap and species from both sides coexist in uneasy richness.

Ecotones are the most biodiverse places on Earth precisely because they belong to neither world fully. They are the edges, the margins, the in-between spaces where adaptation is mandatory and novelty is constant. This chamber celebrates the creative power of boundaries.
