Language Game

Wittgenstein abandoned his early philosophy and started over. Language, he now saw, was not a logical picture of reality but a collection of games — each with its own rules, its own context, its own form of life. The meaning of a word is its use in the language.

Asking what time really means is like asking what a chess piece really does. It depends on the game. In physics, time is a dimension. In conversation, its a greeting. In prison, its punishment. Each use is a different game. There is no single meaning beneath them all.
