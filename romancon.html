The Roman Concrete ChamberChamber 560 of The Liminal Archive

Two thousand years and still standing

Roman concrete — opus caementicium — grows stronger over time. The Pantheon, 2,000 years old, remains the largest unreinforced concrete dome ever built. Modern concrete degrades after 50 years. The Romans mixed volcanic ash with seawater, creating a material that heals its own cracks.

This chamber is built from that concrete. Touch the walls: they are warm, still curing, still strengthening. The microscopic crystals within continue to grow, filling every flaw, making the structure more perfect with each passing century.
