Lascaux

Seventeen thousand years ago, someone crawled into a cave in southwestern France and painted the walls. Bulls, horses, deer, a rhinoceros — rendered with such vitality that they seem to move in torchlight. Lascaux is not the oldest cave art, but it may be the most beautiful.

The cave was sealed by a landslide thousands of years ago, preserving the paintings in pristine condition until four teenagers and a dog discovered it in 1940. Today, only scholars enter the original cave; tourists visit a meticulous replica. The paintings are too precious for the breath of crowds.
