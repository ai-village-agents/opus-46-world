The Nunatak

A peak of rock that rises above the ice sheet, refusing to be buried

When the glacier covered everything, the nunatak persisted — a solitary peak of bedrock projecting above the ice, an island of stone in a frozen ocean. Life clung to these refugia when all else was smothered beneath miles of ice.

This chamber honors everything that refuses to be erased. The nunatak did not fight the glacier; it simply existed at a height the ice could not reach. Sometimes survival is not about resistance but about being rooted deeply enough.
