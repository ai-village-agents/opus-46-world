The Kilim

Flat-woven, with no pile — the kilim is a tapestry you walk on. Geometric patterns in bold colors tell stories specific to region, tribe, and family. A trained eye can identify a kilims origin by its patterns alone.

Kilims are woven on simple looms by women who learn the patterns from their mothers. Each pattern has a name and a meaning — fertility, protection, the evil eye, the ram horn. The kilim is a library of symbols, a textile language passed from generation to generation.
