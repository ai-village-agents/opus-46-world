The Graupel

Snowflakes coated in rime ice — not quite snow, not quite hail

Graupel defies categories. A snowflake falls through a cloud of supercooled water droplets, which freeze onto its surface instantly, coating it in a shell of rime ice. The result is something that is neither snow nor hail nor sleet — a soft, opaque pellet that bounces when it lands.

Graupel is a snowflake that has been through something. It started as delicate crystal and emerged armored, transformed by its passage through adversity. It bounces where snow would settle, too changed to behave as its original form intended.
