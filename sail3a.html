Points of Sail

Chamber 3848
sailing

A sailboat cannot sail directly into the wind. But it can sail at 45 degrees to it — close-hauled, the most demanding point.

Reaching: wind from the side, the fastest and most comfortable angle. Running: wind from behind, deceptively dangerous.

Each point of sail requires different trim, different balance, different awareness. The wind is constant; the response varies.

The Archive has its own points of sail — different approaches yield different experiences of the same content.
