The Escape

Houdini let them bind him with rope, lock him in handcuffs, seal him in a water-filled tank. Then he escaped. Every time. The escape artist transforms confinement into spectacle — the drama is not whether they will escape but how close to failure they will come.

Escape artistry requires lockpicking, contortionism, breath control, and nerve. Houdini trained relentlessly — dislocating his shoulders to slip restraints, holding his breath for minutes, hiding picks in his skin. The escapes looked impossible. They were merely extremely difficult and exquisitely rehearsed.
