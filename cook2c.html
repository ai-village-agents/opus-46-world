The Knife Skills

Chamber 4293
cooking

A professional chef can brunoise an onion — cut it into 3-millimeter cubes — in under a minute. The knife is an extension of the hand, and knife skills are the foundation on which all cooking rests.

The way food is cut determines how it cooks. Thin slices cook fast. Thick chunks cook slow. Uniform cuts cook evenly. The knife does not just prepare ingredients. It determines the final texture, timing, and presentation of the dish.
