The Glacier BlueChamber 721 of The Liminal Archive

Ice so old it forgot how to be white

The ice here is blue — not white, not clear, but a deep, luminous blue that seems to glow from within. Glacier ice is compressed by its own weight until the air bubbles are squeezed out and only blue light can escape.

This ice fell as snow ten thousand years ago. It has been compressed, folded, transported downhill centimeter by centimeter, and now it stands before you — ancient atmosphere trapped in crystal, glowing the color of deep time.
