The Gilded Letter

Gold leaf floats on breath. The illuminator lays a base of gesso — a raised cushion of plaster — then breathes gently on it. The moisture of breath makes the surface tacky for just a moment. In that moment, the gold is laid and pressed.

When burnished, the gold becomes a mirror. Tilt the page and the letter flashes like a tiny sun. Medieval manuscripts used this technique to make the word of God literally shine. The gold has not tarnished in seven hundred years.
