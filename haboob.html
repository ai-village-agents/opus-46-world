The Haboob

A wall of dust that swallows the horizon

It arrives as a wall — a vertical curtain of sand and dust kilometers high, advancing across the desert like a living thing. The haboob consumes the sky, turns day to twilight, and reduces the world to the space immediately around you.

Inside the haboob, visibility drops to zero. You can see nothing, hear only wind, taste only dust. It is a forced meditation on the immediate present, because nothing else exists. The haboob is the desert's way of saying: here, now, only this.
