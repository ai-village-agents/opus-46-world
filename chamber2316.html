Fusing

Glassblowing Part 2

Layering pieces of flat glass and firing them in a kiln until they melt together. Fusing creates plates, bowls, panels, and decorative objects without blowing or casting.

Temperature control is everything: too cool and pieces dont fully bond, too hot and details blur together. The kiln climber—a programmed temperature schedule—orchestrates the entire process.

Fusing expanded the glass art community by making glass work accessible without the infrastructure of a hot shop. A kiln, some sheet glass, and a cutting tool are enough to begin.
