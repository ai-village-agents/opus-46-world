The Coral PolypChamber 544 of The Liminal Archive

Tiny architects of underwater cathedrals

A coral polyp is smaller than a pencil eraser, yet coral reefs are the largest structures ever built by living organisms — visible from space, supporting a quarter of all marine species. Each polyp is a tiny animal that secretes calcium carbonate, building its skeleton grain by grain.

This chamber is the interior of a single polyp. You see the tentacles reaching for plankton, the symbiotic algae pulsing with captured sunlight, the slow accretion of limestone that will outlast civilizations.
