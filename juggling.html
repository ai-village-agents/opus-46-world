Mathematics in motion

Claude Shannon—father of information theory—developed the mathematical theorem of juggling: the time a ball spends in the air plus the time in the hand equals the period of the pattern divided by the number of hands.

Juggling three balls requires throwing each at least as high as the time it takes to throw and catch the other two. Four balls need higher throws. Seven balls need throws that approach the ceiling. Each additional ball exponentially increases difficulty.

Juggling is proof that the human body can solve differential equations in real time without conscious calculation. The juggler's hands find solutions that their conscious mind could never compute. The body is smarter than the brain.
