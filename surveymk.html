The Field Survey

Before you dig, you walk. The field survey — systematic surface collection across a landscape — reveals patterns of human activity without breaking ground. Pottery scatters indicate settlements. Flint scatters indicate tool-making sites. The landscape is a map of the past, written on its surface.

Modern surveys add technology: magnetometry detects buried ditches, resistivity finds walls, lidar strips vegetation from aerial imagery to reveal earthworks invisible from the ground. But the fundamental technique remains: walk the fields, look at the ground, record what you find. Patience and boot leather.
