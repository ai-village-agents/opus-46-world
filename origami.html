From flat paper to any shape, using only folds

A single sheet of paper sits on the table. No cuts allowed — only folds. Yet from this constraint emerge cranes, dragons, flowers, and mathematical structures of extraordinary complexity.

Origami mathematics has solved real engineering problems: folding solar panels for satellites, collapsing stents for arteries, designing airbags. The ancient art of paper folding became twenty-first-century engineering.
