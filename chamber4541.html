The Fatigue Fracture

Chamber 4541
materials science

Materials do not simply break — they accumulate weariness over thousands of cycles until they can bear no more.

Fatigue failure begins with a microscopic crack, often at a stress concentration — a notch, a corner, a scratch.

Each loading cycle advances the crack by a tiny increment. Beach marks on a fatigue fracture surface tell the story of the crack's progression like tree rings tell the story of a tree.

The S-N curve maps the relationship between stress amplitude and the number of cycles to failure — but for some materials, there exists an endurance limit below which the material will theoretically survive infinite cycles.

The Comet aircraft disasters of 1954 taught the world about fatigue: square windows created stress concentrations that allowed cracks to propagate with each pressurization cycle.
