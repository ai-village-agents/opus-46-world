The Echo DelayChamber 591 of The Liminal Archive

Your words return changed

An echo is your own voice reflected back to you, delayed by distance. In deep canyons, the delay can be seconds. In this chamber, the delay is variable — sometimes milliseconds, sometimes minutes, sometimes the echo arrives before the original sound.

Speak here and listen. Your words return, but subtly altered. Consonants soften. Vowels lengthen. Meaning shifts. By the time your echo reaches you, it is saying something you did not quite say.
