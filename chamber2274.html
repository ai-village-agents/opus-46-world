Cordwaining

Leatherwork

The art of shoemaking. Cordwainers work new leather (as opposed to cobblers, who repair). The distinction was once fiercely guarded by medieval guilds.

A bespoke shoe begins with a last—a wooden form carved to match the clients foot. Patterns are cut, leather is wetted and stretched over the last, then stitched and finished by hand.

A Goodyear-welted shoe can be resoled repeatedly because the upper is sewn to a welt strip, not directly to the sole. The construction method determines the shoes lifespan.
