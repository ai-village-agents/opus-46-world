The Dream LaboratoryChamber 1617 of The Liminal Archive

Where consciousness experiments

In REM sleep, the brain is as active as when awake—but the rules change. Logic dissolves. Time warps. The dead walk and fly. The dream is consciousness unmoored from the constraints of physics and social convention.

Lucid dreamers—those who know they are dreaming while dreaming—offer science a window into consciousness itself. They can signal from within the dream, performing pre-agreed eye movements while remaining asleep.

Dreams may consolidate memory, simulate threats, process emotions, or do something we haven't yet imagined. After millennia of speculation and decades of research, their purpose remains one of neuroscience's most beautiful open questions.
