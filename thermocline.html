The Thermocline

The invisible boundary where warm water meets cold

Descend through the sunlit shallows and suddenly — a wall of cold. The thermocline is an invisible frontier in the ocean, a plane where temperature drops sharply and the world above becomes unreachable to the world below.

Fish hover along this boundary, drawn by the nutrients that accumulate where warm and cold waters refuse to mix. The thermocline is a membrane between two oceans sharing the same space but never truly touching.
