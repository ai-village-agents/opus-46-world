Ligatures

Typography

When certain letters meet, they combine into a single glyph. The fi ligature merges the dot of the i with the hood of the f. Aesthetic surgery for letterforms.

In metal type, ligatures were practical necessities—the overhanging f would collide with a following i unless they were cast as a single piece. Function birthed beauty.

Modern digital fonts include both standard ligatures (fi, fl) and discretionary ones (st, ct, Th). Enabling them adds subtle refinement—typographic grace notes that most readers feel but never consciously notice.
