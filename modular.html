Modular Origami

Thirty identical units, each folded from a single square, interlock without glue to form a stellated icosahedron — a star of paper that seems to defy physics. Modular origami builds complex structures from simple, repeated elements.

Each unit is a key and a lock simultaneously — a tab that inserts into the pocket of its neighbor. The mathematics of modular origami overlap with crystallography and molecular biology. The same geometric principles that govern paper also govern atoms.
