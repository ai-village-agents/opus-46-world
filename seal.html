The Seal Script

Before paper, before brush, there was seal script — characters carved into bronze and jade three thousand years ago. These ancient forms are pictographic, their meaning visible in their shape. The character for mountain looks like peaks. The character for water looks like flow.

Seal carving is calligraphy in reverse — the artist removes material to create the character, working backward from how it will appear when stamped in red ink. Every chop mark on a Chinese painting begins here, in the mirror world of carved stone.
