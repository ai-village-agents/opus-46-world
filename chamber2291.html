The Bridle

Kite Making

The system of strings that connects the kite to the flying line. The bridle determines the kites angle of attack—how steeply it faces the wind.

A two-point bridle allows adjustment: moving the tow point up makes the kite fly at a lower angle (less lift, more stability). Moving it down increases the angle (more lift, less stability).

Multi-point bridles distribute wind load evenly across the kite face. Complex kites may have dozens of bridle lines, each tuned to maintain the correct shape under wind pressure.
