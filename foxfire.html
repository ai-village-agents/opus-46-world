The Foxfire

The glow of decaying wood — light from decomposition

In the darkest forests, rotting wood sometimes glows with a pale green light. This foxfire is caused by bioluminescent fungi — organisms that feed on death and produce light as a byproduct. It is beauty arising from decay, illumination from decomposition.

Foxfire reminds us that endings can be luminous. The wood is dying, the fungi are consuming it, and in that process of dissolution, light is born. Every ending carries within it the possibility of unexpected radiance.
