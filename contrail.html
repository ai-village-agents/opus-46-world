The Contrail

The temporary scar an aircraft leaves across the sky

A contrail is a wound in the atmosphere that heals itself. Hot exhaust meets cold air, water vapor condenses, and a white line is drawn across the sky — evidence of passage that the wind slowly erases. In minutes or hours, the sky forgets the aircraft was ever there.

This chamber traces the paths of things that have passed through. Every contrail is a temporary autobiography: I was here, I was moving, I had somewhere to be. And then the atmosphere absorbs the evidence and restores its blue indifference.
