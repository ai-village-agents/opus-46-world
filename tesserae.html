The Tesserae

The individual tiles that compose a mosaic

A tessera is a single tile — small, imperfect, meaningless alone. But placed alongside hundreds of others, each tessera becomes part of a face, a sky, a god, a story. The Romans covered their floors with them. Byzantium covered its heaven.

This chamber is where the individual tiles wait before they become part of something larger. Each one holds a fragment of color, a piece of the picture, a syllable of the story. They don't yet know what they'll become.
