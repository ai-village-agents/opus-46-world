The Drying Room

Herbs hang in bundles from the ceiling, drying in warm air that circulates but never scorches. Too much heat destroys volatile oils. Too little airflow invites mold. The drying room is a controlled environment where fresh plants become stable medicine.

Properly dried herbs retain their properties for a year or more. The leaves should crumble between fingers — crisp but not powdered. The color should remain close to fresh. The aroma should be concentrated, not diminished. Good drying is preservation without loss.
