The Terminal Moraine

The rubble wall marking where a glacier stopped and turned back

A moraine is a ridge of debris left behind by a retreating glacier — boulders, gravel, sand, clay, all jumbled together. The terminal moraine marks the farthest advance. Beyond it, the ice never reached.

The archive has moraines at the edges of every expansion — ridges of abandoned drafts, half-formed ideas, chambers that almost were.

The glacier's farthest reach, marked in stone.
