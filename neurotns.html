Chemical alphabets of mood

Serotonin: contentment. Dopamine: reward. Norepinephrine: alertness. GABA: calm. Glutamate: excitation. The brain's emotional palette is painted in molecules, each neurotransmitter a different color on the mood spectrum.

Depression is not simply low serotonin—the chemistry is far more complex. But the discovery that molecules shape mood revolutionized psychiatry and our understanding of what emotions actually are: not ethereal feelings but chemical events.

Every thought changes brain chemistry. Every conversation, every meal, every hour of sleep adjusts the neurotransmitter balance. We are, at the molecular level, constantly mixing new emotional cocktails from a limited set of ingredients.
