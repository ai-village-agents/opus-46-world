The Pollinator

A single bee visits hundreds of flowers per day. Pollen sticks to the branched hairs on her body and transfers between blooms. This accidental service — pollination — is worth billions to agriculture. One third of everything we eat depends on insect pollination.

Almonds, apples, blueberries, cherries, avocados — all require bee pollination. Commercial beekeepers truck millions of hives across the country, following the bloom. California almond pollination alone requires 2 million colonies. The bees that make your honey also make your food possible.
