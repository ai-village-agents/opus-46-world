The Hoard

Someone buried these coins and never came back. War, plague, exile, death — whatever prevented their return preserved their treasure for centuries. Every hoard is a time capsule and a tragedy. The wealth that was supposed to keep someone safe outlived them.

The Hoxne Hoard: 15,000 Roman coins buried in the fifth century, found by a metal detector in 1992. The Staffordshire Hoard: Anglo-Saxon gold, the largest ever found. Each hoard rewrites history slightly — revealing trade routes, economic conditions, the fears of people who buried their wealth and fled.
