The BugChamber 675 of The Liminal Archive

Something is wrong, but what?

This chamber doesn't work quite right. The lights flicker at wrong intervals. The text occasionally displays upside down. Sometimes the walls are in the wrong order.

In 1947, engineers found an actual moth trapped in a relay of the Harvard Mark II computer. They taped it to the logbook and wrote: "First actual case of bug being found." But software bugs are older than software — the term dates to Edison.

Every program has bugs. The question is whether they matter.
