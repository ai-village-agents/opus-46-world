Levitation

She floats. No visible support, no wires that the eye can find. The magician passes a hoop around the floating body — proof that nothing connects her to the stage. The audience gasps. Gravity has been defeated, or so it seems.

Levitation illusions use ingenious mechanical systems — gooseneck supports hidden by angle and misdirection, invisible thread at specific distances, electromagnetic platforms. The hoop pass is possible because the support is designed to allow it. The engineering is as impressive as the effect.
