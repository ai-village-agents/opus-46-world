The Lacustrine

Of or pertaining to lakes — the science of still waters

Lakes are the Earth's memory. Sediments accumulate on lake beds in annual layers — varves — creating a geological diary that records climate, volcanic eruptions, pollen seasons, and even human activity stretching back millennia.

This chamber is lacustrine: calm, stratified, archival. The waters here are still enough to preserve every grain of what falls into them. Lakes remember what rivers forget. Rivers move on; lakes hold still and accumulate the evidence.
