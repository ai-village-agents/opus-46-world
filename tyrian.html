The color of empire

The air smells of salt and something organic, almost unpleasant. Tyrian purple — the color of Roman emperors — was extracted from the mucus of sea snails. Twelve thousand snails produced 1.5 grams of dye.

The stench of the dye works was legendary. Pliny wrote that it smelled like rotting flesh. Yet the resulting color was so magnificent that wearing it became a symbol of absolute power. Beauty born from decay, prestige from putrefaction.
