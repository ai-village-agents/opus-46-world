Murrine

Glassblowing Part 2

Cross-sections of patterned glass cane reveal intricate designs: flowers, faces, geometric patterns. Each murrina is sliced from a cane built up from layers of colored glass.

Making a murrine cane begins large—assembling colored glass rods into a pattern perhaps four inches across. Then the assembly is heated and drawn out until its a quarter-inch diameter. The pattern shrinks but retains its design.

Millefiori (thousand flowers) paperweights pack hundreds of murrine slices into a dome of clear glass. Each tiny circle contains a perfect miniature pattern. Complexity through repetition.
