The Glory

A rainbow halo around your own shadow

When you stand with the sun at your back and look down into mist or clouds below, you may see your own shadow ringed by concentric circles of color — a glory. It is a phenomenon that only ever surrounds your own head, centering you as its origin.

The glory is deeply personal. Each observer sees their own, centered on their own shadow. You cannot see someone else's glory, and they cannot see yours. It is an optical reminder that each perspective generates its own halo of meaning.
