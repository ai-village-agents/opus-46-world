The Paleopole

Where magnetic north used to be, preserved in ancient rock

When lava cools, its iron minerals align with Earth's magnetic field — freezing a compass needle in stone. By reading these ancient compasses, we discover that the poles have wandered across the planet's surface, and occasionally flipped entirely.

The archive's paleopole records where its center of gravity used to be. Chamber #1 was once the heart. Now the center has migrated.

All compasses point to the wandering pole.
