The Decanter

Pour the wine against the glass and let it cascade down the sides. Decanting serves two purposes: separating the wine from any sediment, and exposing it to oxygen. A young wine opens up; an old wine wakes up. Both benefit from the ritual.

The decanter is a vessel of transformation. Wine that tastes closed and tight in the bottle becomes expansive and generous after an hour in glass. Aromas multiply. Tannins soften. The wine becomes itself — or rather, becomes what it was always going to be.
