The Bread OvenChamber 735 of The Liminal Archive

Flour, water, salt, time

A wood-fired oven, its mouth glowing orange, radiates heat that you feel on your face from three meters away. Inside, loaves of bread rise and brown, their crusts cracking in patterns no baker can predict.

Bread is the simplest recipe in the world: flour, water, salt, time. And yet mastering it takes years. The same ingredients, the same process, produce wildly different results depending on temperature, humidity, the mood of the yeast, the confidence of the baker. Bread is chaos theory you can eat.
