The Subway

Chamber 4284
transportation

The London Underground opened in 1863 — the worlds first underground railway. Passengers descended into gas-lit tunnels to ride steam trains beneath city streets. The concept seemed insane. It worked.

Subways move millions of people daily through tunnels their passengers never think about. The system is invisible by design — an entire transportation network hidden underground, working in darkness so the city above can function.
