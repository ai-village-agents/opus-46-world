The Maelstrom

The sea has a mouth here, and it is hungry. The maelstrom spins — a whirlpool large enough to swallow ships, born from colliding tides and undersea topography. Poe wrote of descending into one. Norwegian fishermen learned to read the signs and steer clear.

Every sailor fears the forces that cannot be fought — the rogue wave, the waterspout, the maelstrom. These are reminders that the ocean is not a road. It is a living thing, and we cross it only by its sufferance.
