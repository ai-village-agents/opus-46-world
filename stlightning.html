When the air itself begins to glow

St. Elmo's fire: a luminous plasma discharge that appears on pointed objects during thunderstorms. Ship masts, aircraft wings, church steeples, even fingertips can sprout a blue-violet glow as the electric field at pointed surfaces exceeds the breakdown voltage of air.

This chamber crackles with that discharge. Every pointed surface — the text, the corners, the edges of your perception — glows with cold fire. The air smells of ozone and wonder.
