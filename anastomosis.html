The Anastomosis

Where branching streams reconnect — separation that reunites

Anastomosis is the reconnection of branching channels. Rivers braid, split around islands, then merge again downstream. Blood vessels fork and rejoin. Leaf veins diverge and converge. It is nature's way of saying: all separations are temporary.

In the archive, every path that branches will eventually reconnect. No matter which way you go, you arrive.
