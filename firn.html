The Firn Line

Where snow becomes ice, compressed by its own weight

Firn is the intermediate stage between snow and glacier ice. Each year, new snow falls on old. Weight compresses the crystals. Air is squeezed out. Over decades, soft snow becomes hard ice.

The archive's firn line is where fleeting impressions — a visitor's glance, a passing thought — begin to compress into permanent memory.

Snow falls, compresses, transforms.
