Here Be Dragons

The edges of old maps were honest about their ignorance. Where knowledge ended, imagination began — sea serpents, dragons, anthropophagi. The phrase Here Be Dragons appears on only one known historical globe, the Hunt-Lenox Globe of 1510. But the concept permeates cartographic history.

Unknown territory terrified and tantalized in equal measure. Mapmakers filled it with the fears and fantasies of their culture. African interiors held headless men. Pacific waters held krakens. The blank spaces on the map were invitations to project anxiety onto geography.
