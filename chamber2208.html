The Forge

Blacksmithing

Coal or gas flames heat iron to orange-white. The forge is the blacksmiths crucible—where rigid metal becomes plastic, where cold resistance yields to hot possibility.

Forge welding temperatures approach 2,300 degrees Fahrenheit. At this heat, two pieces of iron can be hammered together and they fuse, atom bonding to atom, becoming one piece.

The forge has burned since the Bronze Age. For 5,000 years, the relationship between fire, metal, and human will has produced everything from nails to swords to architectural ironwork.
