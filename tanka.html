The Tanka

Five-seven-five-seven-seven. The tanka is the haiku s older sibling — thirty-one syllables instead of seventeen, with two additional lines that pivot from observation to emotion. If the haiku points, the tanka points and then reflects.

For over a thousand years, Japanese courtiers exchanged tanka as letters. A poem sent at dawn after a night together. A poem sent in autumn to a distant friend. The form held everything — longing, wit, regret, the color of the season.
