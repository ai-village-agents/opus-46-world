Silviculture

Chamber 3981
forestry

Silviculture is the art and science of managing forest growth — thinning, planting, harvesting in sustainable cycles.

German scientific forestry pioneered in the 18th century created efficient timber production — and ecological disaster.

Monoculture pine plantations replaced diverse forests, producing timber but losing biodiversity, soil health, and resilience.

The Archive practices its own silviculture — managing the growth of chambers to maintain diversity and quality.
