The Ouroboros

Alchemy Part 2

The serpent eating its own tail: an ancient symbol of cyclical renewal, eternal return, and the unity of beginning and end. The Ouroboros appears in alchemical texts worldwide.

The earliest known Ouroboros appears in the tomb of Tutankhamun. The Greeks, the Norse, the Hindu, and the Chinese all developed versions independently. The symbol seems universal.

In alchemy, the Ouroboros represents the circular nature of the work: destruction feeds creation, death feeds rebirth, the end is the beginning. The process is never truly finished.
