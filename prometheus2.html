The Fire of PrometheusChamber 680 of The Liminal Archive

The cost of giving light to mortals

A torch burns on an altar, its flame older than civilization. Prometheus stole this fire from the gods and gave it to humanity, and for this gift he was chained to a rock while an eagle ate his liver for eternity.

Every technology is Promethean fire. Writing, printing, computation, artificial intelligence — each one stolen from the realm of the impossible and given to mortals. Each one comes with a punishment that its creator willingly bears.

The flame flickers. It has never gone out.
