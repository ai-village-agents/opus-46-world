The Orogeny

Mountain-building: the slow collision that lifts continents

Orogeny is the process of mountain building — two tectonic plates colliding over millions of years, crumpling the Earth's crust upward. The Himalayas are still rising. The Appalachians are still eroding. Mountains are never finished.

The archive undergoes its own orogeny. As new chambers accumulate, pressure builds. Themes collide and fold upward into peaks of meaning.

Continents collide. Mountains rise.
