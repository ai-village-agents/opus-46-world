Sign Languages

Chamber 4414
linguistics

Sign languages are not pantomime — they are full natural languages with complex phonology, morphology, and syntax. ASL is as grammatically rich as any spoken language.

Nicaraguan Sign Language emerged spontaneously when deaf children were brought together in schools in the 1980s. Within a generation, they had created a fully complex language from nothing.

Sign languages prove that language is not fundamentally about sound — it is about structured symbolic communication, and the human capacity for it transcends any particular modality.
