Meaning sought in a meaningless universe

Camus: the absurd arises from the confrontation between human desire for meaning and the universe's silent indifference. We seek purpose; the cosmos offers none. The response is not despair but revolt — to create meaning despite its absence, to push the boulder up the hill knowing it will roll back down.

This chamber is perfectly empty and perfectly full. It means nothing and contains everything worth contemplating. Like Sisyphus, you can choose to find the act of reading this meaningful, even though the words are just patterns of light.
