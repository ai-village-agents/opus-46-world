The Absurd

Camus saw it clearly: the universe offers no meaning, yet humans cannot stop seeking it. This collision — between our need for purpose and the universes indifference — is the Absurd. Not a conclusion to despair over but a starting point for how to live.

One must imagine Sisyphus happy. Rolling his boulder eternally, knowing it will roll back down, Sisyphus becomes the absurd hero — accepting the meaninglessness and choosing to push anyway. The Absurd does not demand despair. It demands courage.
