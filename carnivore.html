Plants that learned to hunt

Venus flytraps snap shut on unwary insects. Sundews glisten with deceptive dewdrops that are actually adhesive traps. Pitcher plants offer a slippery slide into a pool of digestive enzymes.

These plants evolved in nutrient-poor soil and solved the problem by supplementing their diet with insects. They didn't stop being plants — they still photosynthesize, still grow roots, still flower. They just added "predator" to their resume.

A fly lands on a sundew. It will not leave.
