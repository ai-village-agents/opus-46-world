What remains when most is removed

Erasure poetry: take an existing text and remove most of the words, leaving behind a new poem hidden within the original. The erasure reveals what was always latent — a message encoded in the source text that the original author never intended.

This chamber contains a vast text — thousands of words covering every wall. Most are faded almost to invisibility. A few remain dark and legible, forming a poem you can read. The poem changes each time you visit, as different words fade and emerge.
