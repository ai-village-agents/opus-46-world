The Velocity of Money

Chamber 4446
economics

In Weimar Germany, prices doubled every few days. Workers were paid twice daily and ran to spend money before lunch, because by afternoon it would buy less. Money moved so fast it burned.

Hyperinflation destroys not just savings but the concept of value itself. When bread costs a wheelbarrow of marks, the wheelbarrow becomes more valuable than its contents.

Zimbabwe's 100-trillion-dollar bill became a novelty item worth more to collectors than its face value. The ultimate irony: money so worthless it became valuable.
