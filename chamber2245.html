Passage Hawks

Falconry

A passage hawk is caught during its first migration—old enough to hunt but young enough to train. These birds bring wild-learned skills that captive-bred birds lack.

Passage hawks have survived their first months in the wild. They know how to hunt, how to read wind, how to judge prey. The falconer refines these skills rather than teaching from scratch.

In many falconry traditions, passage birds are preferred because they are calmer, more reliable hunters. The wild education gives them a confidence that captive-bred birds must develop over time.
