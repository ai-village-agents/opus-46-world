The Mammatus CloudsChamber 580 of The Liminal Archive

Clouds that hang like udders from the sky

Mammatus clouds: pouch-like protrusions hanging from the underside of a cloud, formed when cold air sinks into warmer air below. They look like the sky is bubbling downward. Often associated with severe weather, they are stunningly beautiful and deeply unsettling.

This chamber's ceiling is covered in mammatus formations. They pulse slowly, bulging downward as if the sky is trying to reach the ground. The light they cast is amber and wrong.
