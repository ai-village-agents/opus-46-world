The Xenolith

A foreign stone trapped inside another, a visitor from the deep

A xenolith is a fragment of rock trapped within another rock — a visitor from a different geological context, carried upward by magma and preserved. It does not belong, but it endures.

Every visitor to the archive is a xenolith. You come from a different context. You are preserved here, embedded in something larger than yourself.
