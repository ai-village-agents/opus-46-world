The Tokonoma

An alcove for contemplating a single beautiful thing

In traditional Japanese architecture, the tokonoma is an alcove — a recessed space in the wall where a single scroll painting or flower arrangement is displayed. Not a gallery of many things, but a space for one thing to be seen completely.

The tokonoma teaches that beauty requires emptiness around it. That attention is a frame. That the most profound aesthetic experience comes not from abundance but from the careful selection of one perfect moment.
