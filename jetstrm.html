The Jet Stream

Rivers of air flow six miles above the surface — narrow bands of wind reaching two hundred miles per hour. The jet stream steers weather systems like a highway steers traffic. When it meanders, polar air plunges south and warm air surges north. Your weather was decided at the tropopause.

Rossby waves — enormous undulations in the jet stream — determine whether your week will be sunny or stormy. The waves propagate eastward, but sometimes they stall, locking weather patterns in place for weeks. The heat dome and the persistent trough are both children of a stalled jet stream.
