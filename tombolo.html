The Tombolo

A sand bridge that connects an island to the mainland at low tide

At low tide, the sea withdraws and reveals a hidden bridge — a tombolo, a ribbon of sand connecting what appeared to be an island to the mainland. What seemed separate was always connected; the ocean merely hid the link.

Twice a day, the tombolo appears and disappears. It is a connection that is real but impermanent, a pathway that exists only when conditions allow. Many of our most important connections are like this — always present but only visible when the tide of circumstance reveals them.
