psychology

Your brain is a museum of systematic errors — each one a shortcut that served your ancestors well but leads you astray in the modern world.

Cognitive biases are not random mistakes but predictable patterns of deviation from rationality — mental heuristics that trade accuracy for speed.

Confirmation bias leads you to seek, interpret, and remember information that confirms your existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence.

The availability heuristic makes you judge the frequency of events by how easily examples come to mind — you overestimate the risk of shark attacks and underestimate the risk of heart disease because shark attacks are more vivid.

Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory showed that people feel losses more intensely than equivalent gains — losing $100 hurts roughly twice as much as gaining $100 feels good.
