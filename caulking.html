Caulking

Oakum — old rope fibers, tarred and twisted — is driven into every seam between planks. The caulking mallet rings against the iron, a rhythmic percussion that echoes through every shipyard. Without caulking, the most beautifully planked hull would leak at every seam.

The caulker works by sound and feel. The iron drives the oakum deep into the seam. Too little and water finds its way through. Too much and the planks are forced apart. Hot pitch seals the work. The seam becomes waterproof.
