The Veil of Ignorance

John Rawls proposed a thought experiment: design a just society from behind a veil of ignorance — not knowing whether you will be rich or poor, healthy or disabled, talented or ordinary, male or female, any race or any nationality. What rules would you choose?

Behind the veil, self-interest becomes universal interest. You would not design a society that oppresses women if you might be a woman. You would not tolerate poverty if you might be poor. The veil strips away privilege and forces you to design for everyone, because you might be anyone.
