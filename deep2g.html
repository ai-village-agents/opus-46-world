The Submarine Canyon

Chamber 4247
deep sea

Submarine canyons dwarf their terrestrial counterparts. The Zhemchug Canyon in the Bering Sea is deeper than the Grand Canyon and twice as wide. It was carved by underwater landslides and turbidity currents.

These canyons are highways for sediment and nutrients flowing from continental shelves to the deep ocean. They shape the topography of the seafloor as dramatically as rivers shape the land — but in total darkness, unseen.
