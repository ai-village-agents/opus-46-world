The Turbidity Current

An underwater avalanche that reshapes the ocean floor

Triggered by an earthquake or a collapsing shelf, a turbidity current is a dense flow of sediment-laden water that races down the continental slope at highway speeds. It carves submarine canyons and deposits sediment fans hundreds of kilometers wide.

In the archive, turbidity currents carry dislodged memories from upper chambers down into the abyss, reshaping the deep architecture.
