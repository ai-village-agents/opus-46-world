The Wings of IcarusChamber 685 of The Liminal Archive

Fly too close to the sun

Feathers and wax are scattered across the floor. Above, through a skylight, the sun blazes impossibly bright. The chamber tells the story everyone knows: a boy flew too high, the wax melted, he fell.

But consider: Icarus also flew. For a few glorious moments, a human being soared on wings of his own making. Everyone remembers the fall. Almost no one remembers the flight.

The wax is still warm.
