Diffraction

Optics Part 2

Light bends around obstacles and spreads through narrow openings. Diffraction proves that light is a wave—particles would cast sharp shadows, but waves wrap around corners.

Single-slit diffraction creates a pattern of bright and dark bands. Double-slit diffraction creates interference fringes. The patterns reveal the wavelength of light with extraordinary precision.

CDs and DVDs create rainbow reflections through diffraction. The microscopic grooves act as a diffraction grating, separating white light into its constituent colors. Data storage becomes accidental art.
