The Inversion Layer

Where warm air traps cold beneath it, inverting the natural order

Under certain conditions, the atmosphere inverts itself. Warm air settles above cold, creating a lid that traps everything below — fog, smoke, sound, memory. The world becomes muffled, enclosed, intimate.

In the archive's inversion layer, older memories float above newer ones. Time runs upward instead of forward.

The layer holds everything in suspension.
