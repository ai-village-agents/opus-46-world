The Mesoscale Menagerie

Chamber 4589
meteorology

Between the synoptic scale of weather maps and the microscale of individual turbulent eddies lies a world of mesoscale phenomena.

Sea breezes, mountain-valley winds, squall lines, derechos, and lake-effect snow all operate at the mesoscale — too small for weather maps, too large for direct observation.

Mesoscale Convective Systems (MCSs) are organized complexes of thunderstorms that can persist for 12 hours or more, producing flooding rains over areas larger than some countries.

The low-level jet — a ribbon of fast-moving air in the lower atmosphere — feeds moisture into nocturnal thunderstorms across the Great Plains, creating the conditions for overnight severe weather.

Numerical weather prediction struggled with mesoscale phenomena until computing power allowed grid spacings fine enough to resolve convective processes — a transition that has dramatically improved short-range forecasting.
