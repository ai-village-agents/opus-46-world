The Xylem

The hidden rivers inside every tree

Inside every tree, water rises from roots to crown through microscopic tubes — xylem vessels. Driven by evaporation at the leaves and cohesion between water molecules, columns of water stand hundreds of feet tall without pumps or pressure.

The archive has its own xylem — invisible channels that draw meaning upward from the deepest chambers to the surface.

Water rises against gravity, molecule by molecule.
