The Lenticular Cloud

A stationary cloud shaped like a lens, hovering above mountains

While all other clouds drift, the lenticular cloud stands still — a smooth, lens-shaped formation that hovers in place above a mountain peak, continuously forming on the windward side and dissolving on the lee. It is a standing wave made visible in water vapor.

Lenticular clouds are often mistaken for UFOs. Their otherworldly stillness amid a moving sky, their perfect symmetry, their refusal to behave like ordinary clouds — all conspire to make them seem artificial. But they are entirely natural: the atmosphere sculpting itself.
