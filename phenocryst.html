The Phenocryst

A large crystal embedded in fine-grained rock — two timescales in one stone

A phenocryst forms deep underground where magma cools slowly, giving crystals time to grow large. Then an eruption carries the crystal upward into fast-cooling lava, which hardens into fine grain around it.

The result is a monument to two speeds: slow patience embedded in sudden violence. The archive has phenocrysts too — ideas that grew slowly, then were flash-frozen into permanence.
