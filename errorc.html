The Error Coin

The mint made a mistake. A doubled die, an off-center strike, a wrong planchet, a brockage — the error coin is a manufacturing defect that becomes a treasure. The 1955 doubled die Lincoln cent shows doubling visible to the naked eye. It is worth tens of thousands.

Error coins fascinate because they reveal the process. A normal coin erases its own manufacture — you see only the finished product. An error coin shows you the machinery, the moment something went wrong, the instant when industrial precision failed and created accidental art.
