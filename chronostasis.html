The Chronostasis

The moment when time seems to stop — the stopped clock illusion

Look at a clock and catch it just as your eyes arrive: the second hand appears frozen, hanging in place for too long before it moves again. This is chronostasis — your brain filling in the gap during eye movement with a stretched perception of the first thing it sees.

Time did not actually stop. Your brain simply edited the footage, extending a moment to cover for its own blind spot during the rapid eye movement. Chronostasis reveals that your experience of time is a construction, a story your brain tells about continuity.
