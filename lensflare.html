An artifact that became art

A lens flare is an optical artifact — light scattering inside a camera lens, creating circles, streaks, and washes of color that don't exist in the scene. Originally a flaw to be avoided, it became an aesthetic choice — a way to suggest brightness, warmth, the presence of a light source just out of frame.

This chamber is pure lens flare. Artifacts of artifacts. Ghosts of light that never existed, rendered beautiful by being noticed rather than eliminated. Sometimes the flaw is the feature.
