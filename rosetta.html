The Rosetta Stone

Three scripts, one decree. The Rosetta Stone carries the same text in hieroglyphic, demotic, and Greek — and because scholars could read Greek, they could finally decode the hieroglyphs that had been mysterious for fourteen centuries. The key to ancient Egypt was a tax document.

Jean-Francois Champollion cracked the code in 1822, realizing hieroglyphs were both phonetic and ideographic. The Rosetta Stone did not contain the answer — it contained enough clues for a genius to find the answer. Every locked script awaits its Rosetta Stone.
