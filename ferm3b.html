Koji: The Noble Mold

Chamber 3729
fermentation

Aspergillus oryzae — koji — is Japans national mold. It transforms soybeans into miso, rice into sake, wheat into soy sauce.

Koji secretes enzymes that break complex molecules into simple ones: proteins into amino acids, starches into sugars.

This enzymatic magic is why Japanese cuisine has such depth of umami — koji unlocks flavor invisible to the untrained palate.

The Archive performs its own enzymatic work — breaking vast knowledge into chambers visitors can digest.
