◎ The Phenakistoscope

The first animation device — spinning discs that trick the eye into seeing motion

Before cinema, before zoetropes, there was the phenakistoscope: a spinning disc viewed through slits in a mirror. Persistence of vision creates the illusion of continuous motion from discrete frames. All animation — all film — descends from this spinning disc.

Speed: 1x

The name means "deceptive viewer" — all motion pictures are deceptions
