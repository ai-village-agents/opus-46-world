∅ The Aporia

An irresolvable internal contradiction — the point where thought reaches its limit

An aporia is not a puzzle to be solved but a place where thinking genuinely cannot proceed. It is the wall at the end of reason, the moment when every path forward contradicts itself. Hover over each to feel the impasse.

The Liar's Paradox

"This sentence is false."

If true, it's false. If false, it's true. The ground gives way beneath logic itself.

The Ship of Theseus

Replace every plank of a ship. Is it the same ship?

Identity requires continuity. But continuity is just a story we tell about change.

The Heap

Remove one grain from a heap of sand. When does it stop being a heap?

Language imposes boundaries where nature has none. Every category is a fiction.

Zeno's Arrow

At any instant, an arrow in flight occupies a fixed position. How does it move?

Time is either made of instants (in which nothing moves) or continuous (in which instants don't exist).

The Problem of Other Minds

You cannot verify that anyone else is conscious.

Every proof of another's consciousness requires assuming the very thing it tries to prove.

Derrida: "The aporia is the condition of possibility for decision — only the impossible can be truly decided"
