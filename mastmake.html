The Mast

A single tree, tall and straight, or multiple timbers bound together with iron hoops — the mast must support thousands of square feet of sail in hurricane winds. It is the tallest, most stressed component of the ship, and its failure means the loss of propulsion.

The mast steps into a socket on the keel — the mast step — transferring the enormous forces of wind directly to the ships backbone. Mast-making was a specialized trade. The forests of New England and the Baltic were stripped of their tallest pines for Royal Navy masts.
