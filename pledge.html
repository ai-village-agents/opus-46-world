The Pledge

Every trick has three acts. The Pledge: the magician shows you something ordinary. A coin, a bird, a deck of cards. The Turn: the ordinary becomes extraordinary. The coin vanishes. The bird becomes two. The Prestige: the payoff, the restoration, the reveal that brings the audience to their feet.

Christopher Nolans film borrowed these terms from magic theory. Every great trick — and every great story — follows this structure. Show what is normal. Break it. Then reveal a new normal that is more wonderful than the original. The audience does not applaud the method. They applaud the feeling.
