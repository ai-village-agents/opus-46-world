The Last Speakers

Chamber 4522
linguistics

A language dies every two weeks. By 2100, half of the world's 7,000 languages will be extinct. Each death erases a unique way of categorizing experience, a library of ecological knowledge, a universe of metaphor.

Endangered languages often encode knowledge found nowhere else. Australian Aboriginal languages contain directional systems more precise than GPS. Amazonian languages encode evidentiality — you must specify how you know what you claim.

Documentation preserves grammar and vocabulary but not the living practice. A language without speakers is a blueprint without builders. The knowledge of how to live in a specific way, in a specific place, dies with fluency.
