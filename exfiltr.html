Exfiltration

The agent is burned. Their cover is blown. Every minute they remain increases the risk of arrest, interrogation, disappearance. Exfiltration is the emergency extraction — the desperate sprint from danger to safety across borders, checkpoints, and hostile territory.

Tunnels, smuggled compartments, forged documents, diplomatic pouches — exfiltration uses every tool available. The most famous: the CIA s exfiltration of six American diplomats from Tehran in 1980, disguised as a Canadian film crew scouting locations. Reality dressed as fiction.
