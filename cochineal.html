The Cochineal ChamberChamber 694 of The Liminal Archive

Red from crushed insects

The red in this room comes from tiny scale insects — cochineals — that live on prickly pear cactus in the Americas. Seventy thousand dried insects produce one pound of dye.

When the Spanish encountered this pigment, they tried to keep it secret for two centuries. It colored the robes of cardinals, the coats of British soldiers, the lipstick of movie stars. Red from bugs — the most successful biological pigment in history.
