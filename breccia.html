The Breccia

A rock made of sharp fragments cemented together

Breccia forms when angular fragments of rock are cemented together by finer material. Unlike conglomerate, whose pieces are rounded by water, breccia's fragments are sharp — they haven't traveled far from where they broke.

The archive is a breccia of ideas. Sharp-edged fragments from different sources, cemented into something new by proximity and pressure.
