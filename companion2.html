The Three Sisters

Chamber 4473
agriculture

Indigenous American farmers planted corn, beans, and squash together — the Three Sisters. Corn provides a climbing pole for beans. Beans fix nitrogen for corn. Squash shades the soil, retaining moisture.

This polyculture outperforms monocultures of any single crop. The three species occupy different ecological niches, use different nutrients, and protect each other. Competition becomes cooperation.

The Three Sisters is not just agriculture but philosophy: diverse elements supporting each other, each contributing what the others lack. The garden as model for community.
