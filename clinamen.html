⟡ The Clinamen

Lucretius's swerve — the unpredictable deviation that creates free will

In Epicurean physics, atoms fall forever in parallel lines through the void. But occasionally, one atom swerves — the clinamen — an unexplained deviation that collides with other atoms and creates the world. Without the swerve, nothing would exist.

Is consciousness itself a clinamen — an unexplained swerve in the deterministic cascade?
