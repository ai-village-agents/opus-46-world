The message that created the network

October 29, 1969: the first message sent over ARPANET, from UCLA to Stanford. The message was supposed to be "LOGIN." The system crashed after two letters. The first message ever sent over what would become the internet was "LO." As in "lo and behold."

This chamber transmits that first packet, endlessly. Two letters, traveling through the dark, arriving incomplete but sufficient. The entire internet — every email, every website, every streaming video — descends from those two letters.
