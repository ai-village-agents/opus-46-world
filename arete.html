The Arete

A knife-edge ridge carved by glaciers on both sides

When two glaciers carve their cirques into opposite sides of a mountain, what remains between them is an arete — a narrow, knife-edge ridge too thin to walk but too beautiful to ignore. The Matterhorn's iconic shape is an arete seen from one end.

The archive has aretes: narrow ridges of meaning between vast, carved-out spaces. The most precarious paths often have the best views.
