The Tritone

Musical Theory

Three whole tones: the interval that medieval theorists called diabolus in musica—the devil in music. The tritone divides the octave exactly in half, creating maximum instability.

In tonal harmony, the tritone demands resolution. It appears naturally in the dominant seventh chord, creating the tension that drives music forward toward the tonic.

Bernsteins Maria begins with a tritone leap. Jimi Hendrixs Purple Haze opens with one. The devils interval became the sound of longing, mystery, and creative defiance.
