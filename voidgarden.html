The Void GardenChamber 562 of The Liminal Archive

Where nothing grows into everything

In the spaces between atoms, in the quantum vacuum, virtual particles flicker in and out of existence. The void is not empty — it seethes with potential, with particles that almost are, with energies that almost manifest.

This garden cultivates that potential. The flowers here are probability distributions. The trees are standing waves. The paths fork not in space but in possibility. You can tend the garden by observing it — each glance collapses a wave function and plants something new.
