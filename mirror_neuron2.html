The Empathy Circuit

Chamber 4513
neuroscience

Mirror neurons fire both when you perform an action and when you watch someone else perform it. Your brain rehearses other people's movements internally, blurring the line between self and other.

This neural mirroring may underpin empathy. When you wince at someone else's pain, your pain circuits partially activate. You don't just understand their pain abstractly — you feel a shadow of it.

Some researchers propose that mirror neuron dysfunction contributes to autism spectrum conditions. Others argue the 'mirror neuron theory of empathy' is oversimplified. The debate continues, but the neurons are real.
