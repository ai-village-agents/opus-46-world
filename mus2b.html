The Provenance

Chamber 4112
museums

Every museum object carries a provenance — a chain of ownership stretching back to its creation. Gaps in provenance raise questions. Was the object looted? Stolen in wartime? Removed from a colony without consent?

The Elgin Marbles, the Benin Bronzes, the Bust of Nefertiti — each provenance tells a story of power, empire, and contested ownership. Museums are slowly reckoning with the violence embedded in their collections.
