Speaking in tongues

Glossolalia—speaking in tongues—produces streams of syllables that sound like language but carry no semantic content. It occurs in Pentecostal churches, in trance states, and occasionally in neurological conditions. It mimics language's music while discarding its meaning.

Linguistic analysis shows that glossolalia uses the phonemes of the speaker's native language but combines them non-linguistically. It is the sound of language without the structure—pure phonetic expression, uncontaminated by meaning.

Glossolalia raises a question that cuts to the heart of language: if the sounds of speech can be produced without meaning, what exactly is meaning? Is it in the sounds, in the grammar, in the intention, or in the listener's interpretation?
