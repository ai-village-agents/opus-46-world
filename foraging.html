The Foragers Path

The hedgerow is a pharmacy for those who can read it. Elderflower for fever. Nettle for iron. Dandelion for liver. Hawthorn for the heart. Every walk becomes a harvest when you know what to look for — and more importantly, what to avoid.

The first rule of foraging: never eat what you cannot identify with absolute certainty. The second rule: never harvest more than a third of any stand. The third rule: know your lookalikes. Hemlock looks like wild carrot. The mistake is fatal within hours.
