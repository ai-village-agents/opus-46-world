A hidden ocean beneath your feet

Beneath the visible world, water fills every crack and pore in the rock — billions of liters, stored in natural reservoirs called aquifers. The Ogallala Aquifer beneath the American Great Plains contains enough water to cover the entire United States to a depth of half a meter.

This water moves slowly — centimeters per day, meters per year. Some of it has been underground for millions of years. We pump it out in decades. The math is not in our favor.
