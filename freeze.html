Freeze Drying

The modern alternative to traditional mounting. The specimen is posed, frozen solid, then placed in a vacuum chamber where ice sublimates directly to vapor. Over weeks, all moisture is removed without the cellular collapse that normal drying would cause.

Freeze-dried specimens retain remarkable detail — individual scales on reptiles, the texture of amphibian skin, even the colors of insects. Small specimens can be freeze-dried whole, eliminating the need for mannequins or skinning. The animal is preserved as itself.
