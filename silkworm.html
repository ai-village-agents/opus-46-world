The Silk ChamberChamber 1509 of The Liminal Archive

A caterpillar's cocoon becomes an empire's most precious commodity

A silkworm spins its cocoon — a single continuous thread up to 900 meters long. The thread is so fine that 3,000 cocoons are needed for one pound of raw silk.

China guarded the secret of silk production for over 3,000 years. Smuggling silkworm eggs out of China was punishable by death. The technology eventually leaked westward via Constantinople, but for millennia, silk was China's nuclear secret.
