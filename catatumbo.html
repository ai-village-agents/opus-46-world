The Catatumbo Lightning

Eternal lightning over a Venezuelan lake — 260 nights per year

Over Lake Maracaibo in Venezuela, lightning strikes 260 nights per year, for hours at a time, in the same spot. The Catatumbo lightning is caused by winds funneling through the mountains, lifting warm humid air to create an almost permanent electrical storm. It has been happening for millennia.

Sailors used it as a lighthouse. Scientists still debate its exact mechanism. This chamber flashes with the rhythm of Catatumbo — not random, but not regular either, a persistent atmospheric conversation between lake and mountain and sky.
