The Tea Room

Tea Ceremony

Four and a half tatami mats. The tea room (chashitsu) is deliberately small—intimate enough that pretension has no room to hide. Guests must crawl through a low entrance, equalizing all.

The nijiriguchi (crawl-through entrance) was originally designed so that samurai had to remove their swords to enter. The tea room was a space of peace, enforced by architecture.

Natural materials dominate: clay walls, wooden pillars, bamboo lattice, tatami grass. The room itself embodies wabi—the beauty of imperfection, simplicity, and the passage of time.
