The Categorical Imperative

Chamber 4333
ethics

Kant proposed that you should act only according to principles you could will to be universal laws. Lying fails this test — a world where everyone lies destroys the concept of truth that makes lying possible.

The categorical imperative demands consistency: if everyone did what you are doing, would the world still function? It is a powerful filter but a blunt instrument. Sometimes lying saves lives. The imperative does not make exceptions.
