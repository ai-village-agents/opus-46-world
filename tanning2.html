Tanning the Hide

The skin must be preserved or it will rot. Tanning converts raw hide into stable leather through chemistry — salt first, to draw out moisture, then tanning agents that cross-link the collagen fibers. The result is a skin that will last centuries without decay.

For taxidermy, the tanning must be thorough but not excessive. The skin needs to remain flexible enough to stretch over the mannequin and hold fine details — the folds around eyes, the creases of ears, the texture of lips. Too stiff and the detail is lost.
