The Colophon

At the end of the book, the colophon: a statement by the maker. Who printed it, where, when, on what press, with what type, on what paper. The colophon is the books birth certificate — provenance recorded at the moment of creation.

Before title pages existed, the colophon was the only way to identify a book. Early printed books borrowed the tradition from manuscripts, where scribes recorded the date of completion and sometimes a prayer of thanks for having finished the arduous work of copying.
