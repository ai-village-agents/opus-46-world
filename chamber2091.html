Jewelry Making

Tiny spheres of gold fused to a surface without visible solder. The Etruscans mastered this technique around 700 BCE, and their finest work remains unmatched.

The spheres can be as small as 0.2mm. Arranging hundreds of them into patterns requires steady hands, sharp eyes, and a form of meditative patience that borders on trance.

Modern metallurgists have partially decoded the technique: copper salts and organic glue create a eutectic bond at lower temperatures. But the Etruscan secret was their patience.
