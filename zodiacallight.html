The Zodiacal Light

Sunlight scattered by ancient dust between the planets

After sunset or before dawn, far from city lights, a faint triangular glow rises from the horizon along the ecliptic. This zodiacal light is sunlight scattered by dust particles — debris from comets and asteroid collisions — spread along the plane of the solar system.

You are seeing the solar system's own atmosphere, a thin disk of ancient dust illuminated by the sun from below the horizon. It is the faintest, most subtle cosmic phenomenon visible to the naked eye — a ghost of sunlight bouncing off particles older than the Earth.
