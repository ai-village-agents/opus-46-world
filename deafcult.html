Deaf Culture

Deaf culture is not defined by absence but by presence — the presence of a visual language, a shared history, and a community forged in the face of a hearing worlds insistence that deafness is a deficit. Within Deaf culture, deafness is an identity, not a disability.

Deaf schools are cultural hubs — places where children meet others like themselves, often for the first time. The shared language creates shared experience creates shared identity. The capital-D in Deaf is deliberate: it marks a culture, not a condition.
