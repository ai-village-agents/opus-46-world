The Classifiers

In sign language, classifiers are handshapes that represent categories of objects. A flat hand is a vehicle. An upright index finger is a person. A curved hand is a round object. These classifiers can then be moved through signing space to tell stories with cinematic precision.

A signer describing a car accident does not sign CAR CRASH CAR. They place two flat hands in space — two vehicles — and move them together. The spatial grammar allows simultaneous information: location, direction, speed, and impact all conveyed in a single gesture.
