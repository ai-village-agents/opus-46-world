The Ancient Partnership

Falconry

Falconry predates recorded history. Evidence from Central Asian steppes dates to 4,000 years ago. Genghis Khan kept 200 gyrfalcons. Frederick II wrote the definitive falconry text in 1250.

UNESCO recognizes falconry as Intangible Cultural Heritage—practiced in over 80 countries across every inhabited continent. The oldest continuous hunting partnership between humans and wild animals.

The paradox of falconry: the bird is never truly tamed. It hunts because hunting is its nature. It returns because the falconer offers something worth returning to. Freedom and partnership coexist.
