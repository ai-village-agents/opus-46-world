The Oak Barrel

French oak or American? The choice defines the wine. French oak whispers — subtle vanilla, gentle spice. American oak shouts — bold coconut, sweet toast. The barrel is not merely a container but an ingredient, contributing tannins, oxygen, and flavor compounds.

A cooper shapes the barrel from staves split along the grain, bending them with fire and water. The inside is toasted to precise specifications — light, medium, or heavy — each level releasing different compounds. The barrel breathes, and the wine ages.
