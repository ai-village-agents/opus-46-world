Nigredo

Alchemy Part 2

The blackening: the first stage of the Great Work. Nigredo is decomposition, putrefaction, the death of the old form. Everything must be destroyed before it can be reborn.

In the laboratory, nigredo corresponds to heating substances until they blacken and break down. In the soul, it corresponds to confronting the shadow—the dark aspects of the self.

Carl Jung drew extensively on alchemical nigredo as a metaphor for psychological transformation. The dark night of the soul precedes enlightenment. You must go through darkness to reach gold.
