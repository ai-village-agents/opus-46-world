The Dormancy

The art of waiting — life suspended between seasons

Seeds wait in the soil for years, sometimes decades, sometimes centuries. Dormancy is not death — it is life in its most patient form, metabolically suspended, waiting for the precise combination of conditions that will allow germination.

Some seeds require fire to germinate. Some need to pass through the digestive system of a bird. Some wait for a flood that comes once in a generation. Dormancy is a bet on the future — a wager that the right moment will eventually come, and that when it does, life will be ready.
