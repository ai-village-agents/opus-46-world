The Noctilucent Clouds

Ice crystals glowing at the edge of space, visible only in deepest twilight

At the very edge of the atmosphere — 80 kilometers up, where space begins — ice crystals form clouds so high they catch sunlight long after the sun has set for everyone below. Noctilucent clouds glow electric blue against the dark sky, visible only during deep twilight.

These are the highest clouds on Earth, forming in the coldest place in our atmosphere. They are a recent phenomenon, first observed in 1885, possibly linked to the dust from Krakatoa's eruption. They are the atmosphere whispering secrets about its own boundaries.
