The Language of Dance

Chamber 4487
beekeeping

Karl von Frisch decoded the waggle dance in 1945. A returning forager dances on the comb: the angle indicates direction relative to the sun, the duration indicates distance. A symbolic language from an insect brain.

The waggle dance conveys distance, direction, and quality of a food source. Other bees attending the dance can weigh these factors and decide whether the trip is worthwhile. Cost-benefit analysis, performed in the dark, on a vertical surface.

Recently, researchers discovered that the dance also communicates the type of flower — through scent molecules on the dancer's body. The dance is multimedia: geometry for location, chemistry for content.
