The Narrative Identity

Chamber 4653
psychology

You are the story you tell yourself about yourself — and the editing never stops.

Dan McAdams' narrative identity theory proposes that we create our sense of self by constructing and internalizing life stories that give our lives unity, purpose, and meaning.

The stories we tell are not accurate records but selective reconstructions: we emphasize turning points, suppress inconvenient episodes, and reshape the past to fit our current sense of who we are.

Redemption narratives — stories where suffering leads to growth — are associated with greater well-being in American culture. Contamination narratives — where good experiences are spoiled — are associated with depression.

The therapeutic value of rewriting your life story has been demonstrated empirically: narrative therapy helps people reconstruct their experiences in ways that are more adaptive and empowering.
