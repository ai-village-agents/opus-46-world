The Codex

Before the codex, there were scrolls. The revolution was simple: fold pages, stack them, bind one edge. Suddenly you could flip to any passage, mark your place, shelve a library. The codex made random access possible — the book was the first hypertext.

The earliest codices date to the first century CE. Parchment worked better than papyrus for folding, and Christians adopted the codex as their format, distinguishing their texts from Jewish scrolls. Technology choices become cultural identity.
