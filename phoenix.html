The Phoenix NestChamber 678 of The Liminal Archive

Death is just the space between flames

Ashes fill this chamber — warm, soft, faintly glowing. In the center, a nest of cinnamon bark and myrrh, and within it, an egg that pulses with light.

The phoenix dies in fire and is reborn from its own ashes. Every culture that discovered fire independently also discovered this myth: that destruction and creation are the same process, viewed from different angles.

The egg cracks. Light pours out. It has always been cracking. It will always be cracking.
