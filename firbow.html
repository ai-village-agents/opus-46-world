The Fire Bow

Friction creates heat. Heat creates ember. Ember creates fire. The bow drill is humanitys oldest technology — a spindle, a fireboard, a bow, and patience. The coal appears as a wisp of smoke, then a glow, then a tiny miracle of combustion.

Everything depends on the materials. Dry softwood for the fireboard. A straight, hard spindle. A cord that grips without slipping. And the technique — steady, rhythmic strokes of the bow, consistent downward pressure. The fire does not appear for those who rush.
