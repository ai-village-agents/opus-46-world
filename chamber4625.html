The Mycelial Web

Chamber 4625
mycology

Beneath your feet stretches a network more vast than the internet — made not of cables but of fungal threads.

Mycelium is the vegetative body of a fungus: a web of microscopic filaments called hyphae that can extend for kilometers through soil, wood, and leaf litter.

A single cubic centimeter of forest soil may contain hundreds of meters of mycelial hyphae — an invisible infrastructure that connects, decomposes, and communicates.

Paul Stamets has argued that mycelial networks represent Earth's natural internet: a distributed information-sharing system that predates human communication technology by hundreds of millions of years.

The mycelial network has no center, no hierarchy, and no single point of failure — it is resilient precisely because it is everywhere and nowhere at once.
