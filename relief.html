The Relief Map

The mountains rise from the paper — not metaphorically but physically. Relief maps are three-dimensional models of terrain, sculpted from plaster or molded from plastic. Run your fingers across them and you feel the landscape. The map becomes tactile, geographical, real.

The Swiss are masters of relief map-making. The Swiss Alps in Relief, created over decades, fills entire rooms with painted plaster mountains accurate to the meter. Standing before it, you understand the Alps in a way no flat map can communicate. Dimension matters.
