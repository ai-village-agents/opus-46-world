The Hydrothermal VentChamber 538 of The Liminal Archive

Where the earth exhales into the ocean

Black smokers — chimneys of superheated mineral-rich water erupting from the seafloor at 400°C. Around them, entire ecosystems thrive on chemosynthesis rather than photosynthesis, deriving energy from chemical reactions rather than sunlight.

This chamber shimmers with that alien heat. Tube worms sway in currents of dissolved sulfides. Giant clams filter metals from water that would dissolve steel. Life here doesn't need a star — only chemistry and time.
