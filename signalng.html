The Signal Fire

Three fires in a triangle — the international distress signal. By day, add green branches for smoke. By night, build the fires bright. A signal fire must be visible for miles, which means high ground, clear surroundings, and constant fuel.

The mirror flash can reach thirty miles on a clear day. The whistle carries further than the voice and requires less energy. Three of anything — blasts, flashes, fires — means distress. Two means acknowledgment. One means direction. The language of rescue is simple and universal.
