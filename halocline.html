The Halocline

Where salt water meets fresh and they refuse to mix

In certain caves and ocean inlets, a layer of fresh water sits atop salt water. The boundary between them — the halocline — is so sharp you can see it: a shimmering, wavering line where the world splits into two densities.

Dive through a halocline and reality shifts. Light bends differently. Sound travels differently. You are in the same place but a different world.

Fresh above, salt below. The boundary shimmers.
