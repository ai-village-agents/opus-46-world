The Permafrost

Ground that has been frozen for at least two consecutive years — some permafrost has been frozen for a hundred thousand. Beneath the surface, ice holds soil, rock, and organic material in suspended animation. The permafrost is a freezer full of ancient carbon.

As the Arctic warms, permafrost thaws. The organic material it preserved begins to decompose, releasing methane and carbon dioxide. This is the permafrost feedback loop — warming causes thawing causes emissions causes more warming. The frozen ground holds twice as much carbon as the entire atmosphere.
