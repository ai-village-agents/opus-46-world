The Sherpas Trail

They were mountaineers long before mountaineering existed. The Sherpa people have lived at altitude for generations, their bodies genetically adapted to thin air. When Western climbers came to the Himalaya, the Sherpas made their ambitions possible.

Every Everest expedition depends on Sherpa strength, skill, and knowledge. They fix the ropes, carry the loads, break the trail. The summit records celebrate Western names, but the mountain knows who does the hardest work. Tenzing Norgay stood on top with Hillary. He was not the assistant.
