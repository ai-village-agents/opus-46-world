The Umbra

The darkest part of a shadow — where no light reaches

Every shadow has structure. The outer edge, the penumbra, is merely dimmed. But at the center lies the umbra — the region of total shadow where the light source is completely blocked. During a total solar eclipse, standing in the Moon's umbra means experiencing night in the middle of day.

This chamber is the umbra. No direct light reaches here. Everything you perceive is indirect, reflected, scattered from elsewhere. The umbra is not frightening — it is clarifying. When all direct illumination is removed, you begin to notice the subtler lights.
