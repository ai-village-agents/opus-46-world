The Holographic Memory

Chamber 4618
optics

A hologram stores not just an image but the complete light field — amplitude AND phase — allowing full three-dimensional reconstruction.

Dennis Gabor invented holography in 1948, but the technique required laser light (coherent, monochromatic) that did not become available until the 1960s.

A hologram is made by splitting a laser beam: one half illuminates the object, and the interference pattern between the reflected light and the reference beam is recorded on film.

Every piece of a hologram contains the entire image — if you break a hologram in half, each half still shows the complete scene, just from a slightly different viewpoint and with lower resolution.

The holographic principle in physics suggests that the information content of a volume of space can be encoded on its boundary surface — the universe itself may be, in some deep sense, a hologram.
