Where thoughts crystallize in the cold

The Cold Pool

You descend stone steps into a chamber of ancient blue tile. The air is sharp, each breath forming small clouds that drift and dissolve. A rectangular pool dominates the center, its water impossibly clear and still — cold enough to burn.

Frost creeps along the walls in feathered patterns, and where visitors have pressed warm palms against the stone, their handprints remain as melted hollows, slowly refreezing. The cold strips away everything unnecessary. Only clarity remains.
