The GIS Layer

Every point on Earth has a coordinate, and every coordinate can carry data — elevation, temperature, population, crime rate, soil type, flood risk. Geographic Information Systems stack these data layers like transparent maps, revealing patterns invisible to any single layer alone.

GIS transformed cartography from representation to analysis. A map of disease outbreaks overlaid with a map of water sources reveals contamination. A map of poverty overlaid with transit routes reveals access gaps. The questions you can ask of layered spatial data are limited only by imagination.
