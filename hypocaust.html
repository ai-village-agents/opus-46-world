The Underfloor

Beneath the Archive's chambers runs a network of heating channels — the hypocaust. Hot air from a central furnace flows through brick passages under the floors, keeping the rooms above warm enough for words to survive. You stand in the maintenance tunnels, feeling the heat rise through the soles of your feet.

The walls are blackened with soot from centuries of burning. Somewhere ahead, the furnace roars.
