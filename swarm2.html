The Swarm

The colony divides. The old queen leaves with half the workers — a cloud of ten thousand bees pouring from the hive entrance, swirling into the sky, then clustering on a branch in a living, breathing, humming mass. The swarm is reproduction at the colony level.

A swarm is not aggressive — the bees have no home to defend and are gorged with honey. Scout bees dance on the swarms surface, advertising potential new homes. Through a democratic process of inspection and debate, the swarm reaches consensus and flies to its new address.
