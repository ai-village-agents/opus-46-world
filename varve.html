The Varve Record

Each layer is one year, perfectly preserved in lake sediment

In glacial lakes, each year deposits a distinctive pair of layers: light silt in summer when meltwater flows, dark clay in winter when the lake freezes. These varves are nature's perfect calendar — countable, measurable, unbroken for millennia.

The archive keeps its own varve record. Every day of its existence is a layer. Every session, a season.
