The Safe House

An unremarkable apartment on an unremarkable street. The curtains are always drawn. The lease is held by a company that does not exist. Inside, clean passports wait in a drawer. Emergency cash in three currencies. A radio with preset frequencies.

The safe house exists in the gap between maps — a place that is officially nowhere, inhabited by no one. Agents arrive at irregular intervals, sleep for a few hours, exchange documents, and disappear. The neighbors think a quiet tenant travels frequently for work.
