The Pelagic Zone

The open ocean — water with no bottom and no shore

The pelagic zone is the open water column — not the surface, not the bottom, but the vast blue nothing in between. It is the largest habitat on Earth and the most featureless. Here there is no shelter, no substrate, no landmark. Only water extending in every direction.

Creatures of the pelagic zone are perpetual wanderers. There is nowhere to hide, nowhere to rest. They must swim or sink, move or die. This chamber captures that radical exposure — the vulnerability of existing in a space with no edges.
