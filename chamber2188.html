The Pin Tumbler Lock

Locksmithing

Spring-loaded pins of varying lengths. When the correct key is inserted, each pin aligns at the shear line, allowing the cylinder to rotate. Elegant, mechanical, and ancient.

The basic principle dates to ancient Egypt, 4,000 years ago. Linus Yale Jr. refined it in 1861 into the design still used in billions of locks worldwide today.

Each pin stack consists of a key pin and a driver pin. The key lifts each key pin to exactly the right height. Too high or too low and the driver pin blocks rotation. Precision grants access.
