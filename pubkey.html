Public Key

The most counterintuitive idea in cryptography: publish your encryption key for the world to see. Anyone can encrypt a message to you. Only you can decrypt it, because only you hold the private key. The mathematical relationship between the keys makes this possible.

RSA, invented in 1977, relies on the difficulty of factoring large numbers. Multiplying two primes is easy. Factoring their product is hard — astronomically hard, for large enough numbers. Every secure website, every encrypted email, every digital signature rests on this asymmetry.
