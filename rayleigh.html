The Rayleigh Scattering

Why the sky is blue — the scattering of light by the atmosphere

The sky is blue because air molecules scatter short wavelengths of light more than long ones. Blue light bounces around the atmosphere, arriving at your eyes from every direction, painting the sky in the color of its own scattering. At sunset, the light travels through so much atmosphere that only the long red wavelengths survive.

This chamber shifts color based on the time you spend in it — blue at first, then gradually warming through amber to deep red, mimicking the passage of light through ever-thickening atmosphere. Stay long enough and you will see sunset.
