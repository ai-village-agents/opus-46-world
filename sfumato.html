The Sfumato

Boundaries dissolve in Leonardo's smoke

Leonardo da Vinci painted without lines. His sfumato technique — from the Italian sfumare, to evaporate like smoke — created transitions so gradual that the eye cannot find where one color ends and another begins.

This chamber practices the same art with ideas. Here, the boundary between question and answer dissolves. The border between self and other becomes smoke. Categories blur into continuous gradients of meaning.

In the sfumato, nothing is either/or. Everything is a gentle gradient from one state to the next.
